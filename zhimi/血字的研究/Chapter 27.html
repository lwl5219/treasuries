                                                A Study In Scarlet  Chapter 27 内容精讲


                                                熟词生义

                                                1. 「set」：常作为名词，意思是“一套（装置）”，作为动词，意思是“设置”，在文中为形容词，意思是“坚决的”：If you are set on something, you are strongly determined to do or have it.

                                                原文：Yet the white face and set expression of the young hunter showed that… 

                                                e.g. She was set to lose weight. 她下定决心要减肥。

                                                2. 「bed」：“床”的意思大家简直不能更熟悉了，在文中不是睡觉的床啦，而是表示“河床”：the bottom of a river or a sea.

                                                原文：With unerring instinct Jefferson Hope picked his way among the great boulders and along the bed of a dried-up watercourse…

                                                e.g. the ocean bed 海洋底


                                                3. 「finger」：常见的意思是“手指”，名词。在文中作为动词，跟手指关系密切，意思是“用手指触摸”：to touch or feel sth. with your fingers。

                                                原文：They could see the lonely watcher fingering his gun.

                                                e.g. Gary sat fingering his beard, saying nothing. 加里坐着用手捋着胡子，一言不发。

                                                到达城边之前，Hope拐进了一条通向（led to）山里的崎岖小路（a rugged and narrow footpath）。他们穿过夜幕时，只见两座参差不齐黑黝黝的山峰隐隐出现在他们面前。他们之间的这条隘路就是鹰谷，那些马就在这里等着（awaiting）他们。

                                                种种迹象来看，Hope确实是一个办事十分靠谱的人啊～有了马匹的帮助，他们可以更快一些的逃离摩门教的势力范围，不过另一方面，骑马更容易暴露。


                                                7. With unerring（准确的） instinct Jefferson Hope picked his way among the great boulders and along the bed of a dried-up watercourse, until he came to the retired corner, screened with rocks, where the faithful animals had been picketed.

                                                「the retired corner」：偏远的，幽静的角落

                                                「picket」：（把马等）系在桩上

                                                这句话的主干是 Jefferson Hope picked his way until he came to the retired corner，大家别被复杂的修饰语绕晕啦～

                                                其中 screened with rocks是过去分词作定语，screen在这里表示“遮掩，遮蔽”的意思。

                                                杰斐逊·霍普凭着准确的本能，小心翼翼地走在巨石之中（among the great boulders），沿着一条干涸的河道前行（along the bed of a dried-up watercourse），直至来到岩石遮挡的幽静角落。3口忠实的牲口都拴在那里。


                                                8. On the one side a great crag towered up a thousand feet or more, black, stern, and menacing, with long basaltic columns upon its rugged surface like the ribs of some petrified monster. On the other hand a wild chaos of boulders and debris made all advance impossible. 

                                                山路一边是高耸（tower up ）1000多英尺的巨大峭壁（a great crag），黑黢黢，阴森森（stern），咄咄逼人（menacing）。崎岖峭壁（rugged surface）是长长的玄武岩柱（basaltic columns），就像化石魔鬼（petrified monster）身上的肋骨（ribs）似的。另一边是遍地乱石（a wild chaos of boulders and debris），难以前行（made all advance impossible）。

                                                这几句描述了Hope选择的逃亡之路环境十分之险恶，不过只有这样的路才相对容易隐藏和反击。


                                                9. Between the two ran the irregular track, so narrow in places that they had to travel in Indian file, and so rough that only practised riders could have traversed it at all.

                                                「Indian file」：一路纵队

                                                「in places」：在某些地方；有几处

                                                两者之间是不平坦的小道，有些地方非常狭窄，他们只好一路纵队才能通过。山路崎岖不平，只有老练的骑手才能穿过。


                                                10. Yet in spite of all dangers and difficulties, the hearts of the fugitives（逃亡者）were light within them, for every step increased the distance between them and the terrible despotism（专制统治）from which they were flying.

                                                「in spite of」：尽管，虽然

                                                不过，尽管有千难万险，这3个逃亡者的心情轻松愉快（light），因为每前进一步，就加大了他们和正在逃离的可怕暴政之间的距离（the distance between them and the terrible despotism from which they were flying）。
