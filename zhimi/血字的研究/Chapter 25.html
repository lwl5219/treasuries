                                                        熟词生义

                                                        1. 「post」：我们常看到这个单词名词“岗位，邮件”记忆动词“张贴”的意思，在文中的意思是“柱子”：a piece of wood or metal。

                                                        原文：As he approached his farm, he was surprised to see a horse hitched to each of the posts of the gate. 他走近农场时，吃惊地看到门两边的门柱上各拴着一匹马。

                                                        e.g. corner posts 运动场的角杆


                                                        2. 「smart」：常见的含义是“聪明的，智慧的”，在文中作为动词使用，意思是“使疼痛，痛苦”：to feel upset about a criticism, failure…

                                                        原文："You shall smart for this!" Stangerson cried, white with rage. 你会遭到惩罚的，Stangerson大声叫道，气得脸色发白。

                                                        e.g. They are still smarting from the 4-0 defeat last week.他们仍为上星期的4:0 惨败而难过。


                                                        3. 「spirit」：“精神”的意思我们都比较熟悉啦，在文中是动词，意为“神秘带走”：to take sb. away in a quick, secret or mysterious way.

                                                        原文：Others as well known and as rich as himself had been spirited away before now, and their goods given over to the Church. 此前一些和他一样有名有钱的人都被秘密带走，他们的财产都交给了教会。

                                                        e.g. After the concert, the band was spirited away before their fans could get near them.音乐会一结束，乐队没等歌迷靠近就神秘地消失了。

                                                        句子解析

                                                        1. On the morning which followed his interview with the Mormon Prophet, John Ferrier went in to Salt Lake City, and having found his acquaintance, who was bound for the Nevada Mountains, he entrusted him with his message to Jefferson Hope. 

                                                        和摩门教先知会见（interview）后的第二天，John Ferrier就到了盐湖城，找到了要去（be bound for）内华达山区的那个熟人（his acquaintance），托付（entrusted）他给Hope捎封信。

                                                        Ferrier不仅仅是一个坚强的人，也是个行动力十足的人，前一晚确定了离开的计划，第二天便托人给未来的女婿捎信了～


                                                        2. Still more surprised was he on entering to find two young men in possession of his sitting-room. 

                                                        「in possession of」：占有，拥有，这里是说两个年轻人鸠占鹊巢。

                                                        on entering，这里on作介词，“一...就...”，那么这里的意思是，“一进屋就...”。

                                                        那么这句话的意思是：更让他吃惊的是，他走进房里时，发现客厅里有两个年轻人。

                                                        两位小伙居然在主人不在的情况下就登门入室，大肆得不要不要的。


                                                        3. “This here is the son of Elder Drebber, and I'm Joseph Stangerson, who travelled with you in the desert when the Lord stretched out His hand and gathered you into the true fold.” “As He will all the nations in His own good time,” said the other in a nasal（带鼻音的） voice; “He grindeth slowly but exceeding small.”

                                                        这位是Drebber长老的儿子，我是Joseph Stangerson。当上帝伸出手（stretched out His hand），把你们聚拢到真正的羊栏（true fold）时，我们曾经和你们在沙漠上旅行过（travelled with you）。他在繁荣时期（in His own good time）会把普天人都聚拢起来。另一个带着鼻音说（in a nasal voice）。

                                                        “He grindeth slowly but exceeding small.” 这句引自教义本身：尽管他研磨缓慢，但细小入微。


                                                        4. “We have come,” continued Stangerson, “at the advice of our fathers to solicit（恳求）the hand of your daug hter for whichever of us may seem good to you and to her. ”

                                                        “我们来，” 斯坦杰森继续说道，“是听从父亲的建议，来向你的女儿求婚，你和他看一下我们俩谁可能合适。“


                                                        5. During this dialogue, John Ferrier had stood fuming in the doorway, hardly able to keep his riding-whip（马鞭）from the backs of his two visitors. 

                                                        「fume」：冒烟；发怒

                                                        「hardly able to」：几乎不能

                                                        在这场对话中，约翰·费里尔站在门口，怒火中烧，几乎忍不住要用马鞭抽这两个来访者的后背。

                                                        6. His brown face looked so savage, and his gaunt（消瘦的） hands so threatening, that his visitors sprang to their feet and beat a hurried retreat. The old farmer followed them to the door. 

                                                        「savage」:野蛮的，凶狠的

                                                        「spring to their feet」：跳起来

                                                        「beat a hurried retreat」：快速撤退

                                                        他褐色的脸看上去极为气愤，骨瘦如柴的手是那样咄咄逼人，所以两个来访者一跃而起，慌忙撤退。这个老农场主跟到了门口。

                                                        一个spring to their feet和beat a hurried retreat便让我们看到，所谓长老的儿子们不堪一击，徒有虚名。


                                                        7. “The hand of the Lord shall be heavy upon you,” cried young Drebber; “He will arise and smite（惩罚）you!”

                                                        “上帝之手会严惩你（be heavy upon you），” 小德雷伯叫道，“他会起来重惩你的（arise and smite you）。”


                                                        8. He was a brave man, but he trembled at the vague, shadowy terrors which hung over him. Any known danger he could face with a firm lip, but this suspense was unnerving. He concealed his fears from his daughter, however, and affected to make light of the whole matter, though she, with the keen eye of love, saw plainly that he was ill at ease. 

                                                        尽管他是一个勇敢的人，但他对笼罩在（hung over）自己头顶这种隐约模糊的（vague, shadowy）的恐惧却不寒而栗（trembled at）。任何已知的危险，他都可以坚定地（with a firm lip）面对，但这种悬而未决的状态（suspense）让人身心交瘁。然而他还是隐藏了自己的种种恐惧（concealed his fears），不让女儿知道，假装（affect）对整个事件满不在乎（make light of），而女儿那双慧眼（keen eye）却完全看出（see plainly）他忐忑不安（ill at ease）。

                                                        尽管逞一时痛快地赶走了两个小混蛋，但每个人都知道这仅仅是问题的开始而不是结束，结合着上文，之前一些仅仅犯了小错误的人都离奇失踪了，而Ferrier公然抗婚并冒犯长老的儿子还不知道要面临怎么样的惩罚，谁也不知道这手段会是什么，未知的恐惧才真的让人害怕。这一点Lucy同样很清楚。


                                                        9. He crumpled the paper up and said nothing to his daughter, but the incident struck a chill into his heart. The twenty-nine days were evidently the balance of the month which Young had promised. 

                                                        「crumple the paper up」：把纸揉成一团

                                                        「strike a chill into sb’s heart」：令人寒心，害怕

                                                        「balance」：此处为“余额”的意思

                                                        他把纸揉成一团，什么也没有对女儿说，但这个小事件让他胆战心寒。29天显然是Young约定的一个月的余额。

                                                        摩门教的惩罚以Ferrier没有想到的方式呈现了，确实呼应上文摩门教呈现出的神秘气息。


                                                        10. What strength or courage could avail against an enemy armed with such mysterious powers? The hand which fastened that pin might have struck him to the heart, and he could never have known who had slain him. 

                                                        「avail against」：利于对付

                                                        「armed with」：拥有，具备

                                                        「strike him to the heart」：刺穿他的心脏

                                                        「slain」：动词slay的过去式，意思是“杀害”

                                                        什么力量或勇气才足以应对一个具有这种神秘力量的敌人呢？那只钉别针的手可以扎穿他的心脏，而且他永远不会知道是谁杀害了他。
