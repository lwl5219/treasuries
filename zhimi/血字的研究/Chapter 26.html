                                                    熟词生义

                                                    1.「watch」：常见的意思大家都很熟悉啦。在文中的意思是“警戒;守卫”。毕竟“守卫”是需要一直注视着，两种意思有关系，有理有据～

                                                     原文：The more-frequented roads were strictly watched and guarded, and none could pass along them without an order from the Council. 经常出入的大路都有人严加保守，没有四圣会的命令，谁都不能通过。

                                                    e.g. Jose, as usual, had climbed a tree to keep watch. 乔斯像往常一样爬到树上放哨。


                                                    2.「steel」：常作为名词，意思是“钢，钢铁”。在文中作为动词，意思是“使坚定”，steal one’s heart就是坚定决心的意思，这两者还是有关联的，钢铁的特征就是很坚硬啦，可以这么来记。

                                                     原文："We must make our start at once," said Jefferson Hope, speaking in a low but resolute voice, like one who realizes the greatness of the peril（危险）, but has steeled his heart to meet it. “我们必须马上出发”，Hope说，他说话的声音沉着坚定，就像一个人明白有巨大危险，但下定决定应对一样。

                                                    e.g. As she waited, she steeled herself for disappointment. 她等着的时候，就冷了心不指望了。

                                                    5. John Ferrier felt that instant death would be better than the suspense which shook his nerves and chilled his heart. 

                                                    John Ferrier感到，马上死去（instant death）会比这种胆战心惊（shook his nerves and chilled his heart）悬而未决的状态（suspense）的状态好。

                                                    大家可以记住这两个表现一个人心境的短语：shake one’s nerves和chill one’s heart,非常传神和具有画面感。


                                                    6. With a sigh of relief, Ferrier looked to right and to left, until happening to glance straight down at his own feet he saw to his astonishment a man lying flat upon his face upon the ground, with arms and legs all asprawl（匍匐的）. 

                                                    「a sigh of relief」：松了口气

                                                    「happen to」：碰巧

                                                    「to one's astonishment」: 让某人惊讶

                                                    「lie  upon one's face」:俯卧

                                                    until happening to glance straight down at his own feet 其实是until (he was) happening to glance straight down at his own feet, 当状语从句中的主语和主句的主语一致，并且又含有be动词时，可以省略从句中的主语和be动词~

                                                    Ferrier左右看了看，松了口气，随后碰巧低头看了一眼脚下，大吃一惊。


                                                    7. So unnerved was he at the sight that he leaned up against the wall with his hand to his throat to stifle his inclination to call out.

                                                    「at the sight」：看到，看见

                                                    「lean up against」：靠在

                                                    「stifle his inclination to do sth.」：抑制做某事的冲动

                                                    （So unnerved was he at the sight that …）大家不要被这个句子吓到了，调整成正常顺序的句子就是：he was so unnerved at the sight that…他看到这个情景非常害怕以至于…

                                                    依旧是大家很熟悉的so+形容词+that句子结构，之所以把形容词提前，是为了强调Ferrier的害怕程度：得靠着墙，用手捂住喉咙，才没有喊出声来。


                                                    8. His first thought was that the prostrate（伏地的）figure was that of some wounded or dying man, but as he watched it he saw it writhe（蠕动）along the ground and into the hall with the rapidity and noiselessness of a serpent. 

                                                    「rapidity and noiselessness」：迅速无声

                                                    他开始以为这个俯卧在地上的人（prostrate figure）是一个受伤或奄奄一息的人（that of some wounded or dying man），但当他观察时，他看到那个人在地上扭动身体（writhe along the ground），爬进门厅，像蛇一样迅速无声（with the rapidity and noiselessness of a serpent）。

                                                    当Ferrier已经绝望要放弃的时候，Hope再一次像当初及时出现救了Lucy一样来了。


                                                    9. He flung himself upon the cold meat and bread which were still lying upon the table from his host's supper, and devoured（吞食）it voraciously（狼吞虎咽地）. 

                                                    「fling onself upon」：扑在...上

                                                    主人的晚饭还放在桌上没动（were still lying upon the table），他就扑在冷肉和面包上，狼吞虎咽地（devoured it voraciously）吃了起来。


                                                    10. Hope slapped the revolver butt which protruded from the front of his tunic. "If they are too many for us we shall take two or three of them with us," he said with a sinister smile.

                                                    「slap」：拍打

                                                    「revolver butt」：左轮手枪的枪柄，呐，左轮手枪大概长酱紫

                                                    「protrude from」：从…中伸出，露出

                                                    「with a sinister smile」：邪恶地笑

                                                    Hope拍了拍从短上衣前襟露出来的左轮手枪的枪柄：“就是寡不敌众，我们也要干掉他们两三个，”他狞笑道。
