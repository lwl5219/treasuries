                                            熟词生义

                                            1. 「peep」：常见的意思是“偷看，偷窥”，文中的意思是“隐现”：If something peeps out from behind or under something, a small part of it is visible or becomes visible.

                                            原文：In every direction the great snow-capped peaks hemmed them in, peeping over each other's shoulders to the far horizon. 

                                             e.g.The sun peeped out from behind the clouds. 太阳从云层里露了一下脸。


                                            2. 「game」：常见的意思是“游戏，运动”，在文中的意思是“猎物，野味”：wild animals or birds that people hunt for sport or food.

                                            原文：This gave the hunter little uneasiness, however, for there was game to be had among the mountains.

                                            e.g. Hunters are men who shot game for food. 猎人是靠捕猎获取食物的人。


                                            3. 「mouth」：“嘴”的意思大家简直不能更熟悉了，在文中不是脸上的嘴啦，而是表示“入口”：the entrance or opening of sth.

                                            原文：He had now come to the mouth of the very defile in which he had left them.

                                             e.g. By the mouth of the tunnel he bent to retie his lace. 到隧道出入口时，他弯腰重新系好鞋带。

                                            「startled the weary horses into a gallop」：吓得疲乏的马惊跑起来，gallop此处为名词，意思是“疾驰，飞奔”。

                                            恐惧也不完全是幻想，因为这个荒凉的山谷树木浓密，乱石遍地，树木都曾经这样滚下来过。甚至在他们经过时，就有一块巨石轰隆隆滚落下来，刺耳的隆隆声在这寂静的峡谷里回荡，吓得疲乏的马惊跑起来。


                                            5. At a wild torrent which swept out of a ravine they called a halt and watered their horses, while they partook of（分享）a hasty breakfast. 

                                            「call a halt」：停下

                                            「 sweep」：(风、汹涌的海水等)席卷,掠过

                                            「partake of」：分享

                                            他们在峡谷激流奔腾的一个地方停下来饮马，同时也匆匆吃了一顿早饭。


                                            6. At night-time they chose the base of a beetling crag（突起的悬崖）, where the rocks offered some protection from the chill wind, and there huddled together for warmth, they enjoyed a few hours' sleep. 

                                            「the base of」：... 的底部

                                            「protection from」：保护，免受

                                            「huddle together」：挤在一起

                                            夜间，他们选择悬岩下面，那里的岩石可以使他们避开寒风。为了取暖，他们挤在一起，睡了几个小时。


                                            7. This gave the hunter little uneasiness, however, for there was game to be had among the mountains, and he had frequently before had to depend upon his rifle for the needs of life. 

                                            「game」：猎物

                                             he had frequently before had to depend upon his rifle 可以理解为  frequently before he had had to depend upon his rifle，意思是“从前他常常必须依靠他的步枪”。

                                            然而，这并没有使这位猎人有什么不安，因为大山里有飞禽走兽可以猎食。他以前常常不得不依靠那支步枪维持生活所需。


                                            8. Having tethered （拴）the horses, and bade Lucy adieu（道别）, he threw his gun over his shoulder, and set out in search of whatever chance might throw in his way. 

                                            「bid sb. adieu」：告别

                                            「set out」：出发

                                            「in search of」：寻找

                                            whatever chance might throw  机遇抛给他的任何东西，这个说法是不是很形象～命运给了他什么，他就打猎什么。

                                            他拴好马，和露西告别后，跨上步枪，出发去找找，看有什么猎物可打。


                                            9. Weighed down with his burden, and weary from his exertions, he stumbled along, keeping up his heart by the reflection that every step brought him nearer to Lucy, and that he carried with him enough to ensure them food for the remainder of their journey. 

                                            「weighed down」：被…压低

                                            「his burden」：burden本身意思是负担，此处具体指的是上文提到的“trophy”，也就是猎物。

                                            「weary from his exertions」：因努力而精疲力尽

                                             「stumble along」：蹒跚而走

                                            「remainder of their journey」：余下的行程

                                            他背着沉甸甸的猎物，精疲力尽，蹒跚而行，想到每前进一步就靠近Lucy一步，而且他背的食物足够他们剩下的旅途用，便振作起了精神。

                                            Hope在这个逃亡过程中不仅充当了领路人，也是狩猎者-以自己的丰富经验在深山中最终觅得食物，但同时他也是一位正沐浴在爱河里的年轻人啊，所做的这一切也不过是因为爱情。


                                            10. A vague, nameless dread came over him, and he hurried onwards frantically, dropping the precious food in his agitation. 

                                            一种模糊莫名的恐惧（A vague, nameless dread）掠过他的心头，他发疯似地（frantically）向前跑去，焦虑中（in his agitation）扔掉了珍贵的食物。

                                            这几句读来心酸莫名啊，答案昭然若揭～上文也早有暗示：He little knew how far that iron grasp could reach, or how soon it was to close upon them and crush them. 他完全不知道那只铁腕能伸多远，也完全不知道那只铁腕马上就要逼近，把他们打得粉碎。
