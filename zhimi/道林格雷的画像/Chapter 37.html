道林格雷的画像 Chapter 37

【0】 According to the great alchemist（炼金术士）, Pierre de Boniface, the diamond rendered a man invisible, and the agate（玛瑙） of India made him eloquent. The cornelian（光玉髓） appeased anger, and the hyacinth（红锆石） provoked sleep, and the amethyst （紫水晶） drove away the fumes of wine. The garnet（石榴石） cast out demons, and the hydropicus（宝石名） deprived the moon of her colour. The selenite（石膏石） waxed and waned with the moon, and the meloceus（宝石名）, that discovers thieves, could be affected only by the blood of kids. 「waxed and waned」：阴晴圆缺。Wax and wane有“兴衰、盈亏”的意思，因此这里是表示月亮的阴晴圆缺。 「 be affected only by the blood of kids」：小山羊的血会使其失效。“be affected by”是“受···影响”，而这里的kids并不是指小孩，而是指小山羊。
大炼金术家皮埃尔德波尼法斯说，钻石使人隐形，印度玛瑙使人善辩。光玉髓能止怒；红锆石能催眠；紫晶能消除酒气。石榴石能驱魔；一种称为"赫屈罗皮克斯"的宝石会使月亮失色；石膏石会随月亮的盈亏而增减；一种叫"梅洛西亚斯"的宝石能识别窃贼，只有小山羊的血会使其失效。

    【1】 Leonardus Camillus had seen a white stone taken from the brain of a newly killed toad, that was a certain antidote against poison. The bezoar（毛粪石）, that was found in the heart of the Arabian deer, was a charm that could cure the plague. In the nests of Arabian birds was the aspilates（宝石名）, that, according to Democritus, kept the wearer from any danger by fire.
    列昂那达斯．卡米拉斯见过从刚杀的蟾蜍中取出的白色宝石，可用作解毒剂。从阿拉伯鹿的心脏中发现的毛粪石是治疗瘟疫的良药。阿拉伯鸟巢中有一种"阿斯皮莱茨"的石头，根据德莫克里脱斯的说法，戴了它就可以免除火灾。

      【2】 The King of Ceilan rode through his city with a large ruby in his hand, as the ceremony of his coronation（加冕典礼）. The gates of the palace of John the Priest were "made of sardius（红宝石）, with the horn of the horned snake inwrought（镶嵌）, so that no man might bring poison within." 「with the horn of the horned snake inwrought（镶嵌）」：镶嵌着角蛇的角。这一短语中第一个horn是名词，第二个horn是动词，表示“有角的”，而inwrought是后置定语，表示“嵌入的” 「so that no man might bring poison within」：所以没有人可以把毒药带进来。Bring within是“把···带进来”的意思。 Over the gable were "two golden apples, in which were two carbuncles（红宝石）," so that the gold might shine by day and the carbuncles by night. 「Over the gable 」：山墙之上 「in which were two carbuncles（红宝石）」：内有两颗红宝石。这一篇中出现了大量的珠宝器物的名字，小伙伴们是不是晕了呢~ 「so that the gold might shine by day and the carbuncles by night」：金子在白天闪光，红玉在夜间发亮。这句话的and后面省略了shine，可以补充上去。
      锡兰国王在加冕典礼上手捧一颗巨大的红宝石驱车穿过城市。牧师约翰的 宫门是"用红宝石做成的，镶嵌着角蛇的角，使携毒者不得入"。山墙上放着"两个金苹果，内有两块红玉"，金子在白天闪光，红玉在夜间发亮。

        【3】 In Lodge's strange romance 'A Margarite of America', it was stated that in the chamber of the queen one could behold（看见） "all the chaste ladies of the world, inchased out of silver, 「 strange romance」：怪异的传奇小说 「 it was stated 」：据说 「inchased out of silver」：银刻的。这里的inchased表示雕刻，是过去分词做形容词的用法，形容前的ladies。 这里出现的宝石可真多啊，小伙伴千万不要被吓到，只要了解一下就好啦 looking through fair mirrours of chrysolites（橄榄石）, carbuncles（红宝石）, sapphires（蓝宝石）, and greene emeraults（绿宝石）." 「 looking through fair mirrours of ···」：从···镜中显露出来。Look throgh这里是表示“显露出来”。
        洛奇的一部怪异的传奇《美洲的一颗珍珠》提到，在皇后的寝宫里可以看到"世间所有贞洁女子的银镂刻像，对着橄榄石、红玉、蓝宝石和绿宝石的镜子照个不停"。

          【4】 Marco Polo had seen the inhabitants of Zipangu place rose-coloured pearls in the mouths of the dead. A sea-monster had been enamoured（迷恋） of the pearl that the diver brought to King Perozes, 「had been enamoured of 」：迷恋 「 mourned for seven moons over its loss」：哀悼了七个月。Moon也不仅仅指月亮，可以用来指月份。 and had slain the thief, and mourned for seven moons over its loss. When the Huns（匈奴人） lured the king into the great pit, he flung it away— Procopius tells the story—nor was it ever found again, though the Emperor Anastasius offered five hundred-weight of gold pieces for it. 「 lured the king into the great pit」：把国王引诱进陷阱。 「— Procopius tells the story—」：这是一句插入语，往往表示某人说。 「nor was it ever found again」：再也没有找到（这颗珍珠）。这里是一个半倒装的句子，nor后面的be动词提前到主语之前。还原成正常的语序是“it was not ever found again”。小伙伴们可能还要问，为什么要用nor呢？这主要取决于王尔德写作时的目的，可能他想通过使用nor来加强与前句的联系。 「 five hundred-weight of gold pieces for it」：为之付出五百单位的黄金。Weight除了表示重量，也可以表示砝码。这里文章并没有明示这个砝码的重量为多少，因此我们理解为“单位”。其实最好的方式，就是理解为很多很多黄金啦~ The King of Malabar had shown to a certain Venetian a rosary（念珠） of three hundred and four pearls, one for every god that he worshipped.
          马可波罗曾见到日本国百姓把玫瑰色的珍珠放在死者的嘴里。一个海怪迷上了被潜水员取来献给国王皮罗萨斯的一颗珍珠，杀死了窃珠人，并为自己的损失痛悼了七个月。后来匈奴人把国王诱入陷阱时，据普罗科皮埃斯所说，国王扔掉了珍珠。 尽管阿那斯塔西亚斯皇帝出了相当于五百磅黄金的悬赏，却并未觅到那颗珍珠。马拉巴尔的国王曾给一个威尼斯人看过一串由三百零四颗珍珠组成的念 珠，每颗珠代表一个他所崇拜的神。

            【5】 When the Duke de Valentinois, son of Alexander VI, visited Louis XII of France, his horse was loaded with gold leaves, according to Brantome, and his cap had double rows of rubies that threw out a great light. Charles of England had ridden in stirrups hung with four hundred and twenty-one diamonds. Richard II had a coat, valued at thirty thousand marks, which was covered with balas rubies.
            据勃兰托姆所言，亚历山大六世之子，瓦伦提努阿公爵拜见法王路易十二 的时候，坐骑浑身披着金叶，帽子上镶着两排红宝石，光芒四舐。英王查理的马镫挂着四百二十一颗钻石。理查二世有一件外套，满布玫瑰红尖晶石，价值相当于三万马克重的金子。

              【6】 Hall described Henry VIII, on his way to the Tower previous to his coronation, as wearing "a jacket of raised gold, the placard embroidered with diamonds and other rich stones, 「on his way to」：在去···的路上 「a jacket of raised gold」：凸花纹金线上衣。这里的raised指的是凸起的纹路，而gold是指金丝线。 and a great bauderike（饰品） about his neck of large balasses（玫瑰红尖晶石）." 「 and a great bauderike（饰品） about his neck of large balasses」：脖子上有一块大饰品，由大块的玫瑰红尖晶石制成。 The favourites of James I wore ear-rings of emeralds set in gold filigrane（丝线）.
              霍尔描写亨利八世在加冕前去伦敦塔的路上，身穿"凸花纹金丝线上衣，胸牌上饰有钻石和其他宝石，颈项有一大块饰品，缀有巨大的玫瑰红尖晶石"。詹姆斯一世的宠幸们都戴着金丝线缀成的绿宝石耳环。

                【7】 Edward II gave to Piers Gaveston a suit of red-gold armour studded with jacinths, a collar of gold roses set with turquoise-stones, and a skull-cap parseme（头盔） with pearls. Henry II wore jewelled gloves reaching to the elbow, and had a hawk-glove sewn with twelve rubies and fifty-two great orients. The ducal hat of Charles the Rash, the last Duke of Burgundy of his race, was hung with pear-shaped pearls and studded with sapphires.
                爱德华二世赠与皮埃斯·盖维斯顿一副镶着红锆石的赤金盔甲，一个金玫瑰嵌绿松石的肩，以及一顶饰有珍珠的头盔。亨利二世的手套直抵肘部，上面满布珠宝。他的一只猎鹰手套缀有十二颗红宝石和五十二颗大珍珠。 "鲁莽的查理"，他家族中最后一个勃艮第公爵，所戴的公爵帽悬挂着梨子形的珍珠，装点着蓝宝石。

                  【8】 How exquisite life had once been! How gorgeous in its pomp and decoration! Even to read of the luxury of the dead was wonderful.
                  生活曾是多么美妙啊!那种气派，那种装饰多么灿烂辉煌!甚至连读到逝者 的奢华也令人怦然心动。

                    【9】 Then he turned his attention to embroideries and to the tapestries that performed the office of frescoes（壁画） in the chill rooms of the northern nations of Europe. As he investigated the subject— and he always had an extraordinary faculty of becoming absolutely absorbed for the moment in whatever he took up—he was almost saddened by the reflection of the ruin that time brought on beautiful and wonderful things. 「 had an extraordinary faculty of」：拥有特殊的能力。 「 becoming absolutely absorbed for the moment in whatever he took up」：变得极度专注于着手的东西。become absorbed in表示专注于。Take up是表示“着手某事” 「was almost saddened by」：为···感到悲哀 「the reflection of the ruin that time brought on beautiful and wonderful things」：关于时间带给美妙事物的摧残的想法。The reflection of表示“关于···的想法”，that后面的句子是ruin的定语从句，还原成正常语序为“time brought ruin on beaufiful and wonderful things”。
                    后来他的兴趣转向了刺绣和北欧国家寒冷的房间里充作壁的挂毯。他一钻 进这个题目--他总有一种非同寻常的能力，会一时间极度专注于着手的东西--便几乎为这个题目的启示，即时间笋美妙事物带来的摧残，而感到悲哀。

                      【10】 He, at any rate, had escaped that. Summer followed summer, and the yellow jonquils（黄水仙） bloomed and died many times, and nights of horror repeated the story of their shame, but he was unchanged. 「Summer followed summer」：一个又一个夏天过去 「and nights of horror repeated the story of their shame」：恐怖的夜，可耻的事情一次次发生。这里的nights被拟人化，好像在陈述可耻的故事。 No winter marred his face or stained his flowerlike bloom.
                      至少他已经躲过了这种劫难。一个夏天又一个夏天过去了，黄色的长寿花开了又谢谢。恐怖的夜晚，那些可耻的事情仍一次次发生，而他自己却依然未变。冬天并没有损害他的容颜，或是玷污他如花的青春。

                        【11】 How different it was with material things! Where had they passed to? Where was the great crocus-coloured（橘黄色） robe, on which the gods fought against the giants, that had been worked by brown girls for the pleasure of Athena? Where the huge velarium（天幕） that Nero had stretched across the Colosseum（罗马斗兽场） at Rome, that Titan sail of purple on which was represented the starry sky, and Apollo driving a chariot （二轮战车）drawn by white, gilt-reined steeds? 这句话的主干是Where was the huge velarium，只是省略了was一次。王尔德这么写有一种诗歌的味道。后面是and连接的两个成分，分别是“that Nero had”和“that Titan sail···”。第二个成分里又嵌套了一个从句“on which was····” 「that Nero had stretched across the Colosseum（罗马斗兽场） at Rome」：尼禄要铺盖在罗马剧场的那块巨大的（天幕）。这句话是velarium的定语从句。Stretch across是盖在···上面。 「that Titan sail of purple on which was represented the starry sky」：巨型的紫色风帆，上面画着星光闪耀的天空。Titan是“巨大的”意思，来源于古希腊神话中的泰坦巨神。On which从句的语序可以还原为“the starry sky was represented on which”，而which指的是sail。 「Apollo driving a chariot （二轮战车）drawn by white, gilt-reined steeds」：阿波罗驾驶着二轮战车，由两匹马驱使。Steed指的是战马。这一成分与前面的starry sky并列，并不是一个完整的句子。
                        时间给物质的东西带来的影响多么不同呀!这些物质的东西到哪里去了呢?那件橘黄色的大袍，是皮肤黝黑的姑娘为取悦雅典娜而做的，上有众神与巨人搏斗的图案，它在哪里呢?尼禄要铺盖罗马剧场的那块巨大的天幕，那张巨型紫色风帆，上面画着星光闪耀的天空和阿波罗驾着白骏马配镀金缰绳的战车，如今又在哪里呢?

                          【12】 He longed to see the curious table-napkins wrought for the Priest of the Sun, on which were displayed all the dainties（美食） and viands（佳肴） that could be wanted for a feast; 「longed to 」：渴望 「wrought for the Priest of the Sun」：为太阳神祭司所作的··。Wrought是work的过去分词，表示“为···精制” 「on which were displayed all the dainties（美食） and viands（佳肴）」：美味佳肴全描绘在上面。这个定语从句可以还原成“dainties and viands were displayed on which”，而这的which指的是前面的napkins。 the mortuary cloth of King Chilperic, with its three hundred golden bees; the fantastic robes that excited the indignation of the Bishop of Pontus and were figured with "lions, panthers, bears, dogs, forests, rocks, hunters— all, in fact, that a painter can copy from nature"; and the coat that Charles of Orleans once wore, on the sleeves of which were embroidered the verses of a song beginning 这里并没有完整的句子主干，而是短语，只是短语后面有一些从句或分词结构。这里的外套就是道林向要看到的东西之一。「on the sleeves of which were embroidered the verses of a song」：袖子上绣着一首歌。这句话可以还原成the verses of a song were embroidered on the sleeves of which，而which指的是the coat。 "Madame, je suis tout joyeux," the musical accompaniment of the words being wrought in gold thread, and each note, of square shape in those days, formed with four pearls. 「the musical accompaniment of the words」：歌词的音乐伴奏。accompaniment是伴奏的意思。 「and each note, of square shape in those days, formed with four pearls」：这是完整的一句，只是用逗号分开了。这里的note指的是“音符”，这些音符在过去是画成方形的。
                          道林渴望见到那些为太阳祭司编织的奇异餐巾，上面绣有盛宴所需的一切美食和佳肴；想看一看奇尔佩里克王灵柩上的缀有三百只金色蜜蜂的盖布；还有那些激怒了庞脱斯主教的奇妙的袍子，袍子上画了"狮、豹、熊、狗、森林、岩石、猎人等画家所能描摹大自然的一切"；他还希望一睹奥尔良的查理穿过的外套，袖子上绣着一首歌，起句是"夫人，我非常高兴"，配乐的歌词是用金线绣成的，当年画成方形的每个音符由四颗珍珠来代表。

                            【13】 He read of the room that was prepared at the palace at Rheims for the use of Queen Joan of Burgundy and was decorated with "thirteen hundred and twenty-one parrots, made in broidery（刺绣）, 这句话的主干就是“He read of the room”，后面的成分都是修饰的成分。Room后面跟了好几个并列的句子，分别是“was prepared at”，“was decorated with”，“and blazoned with”。Whose wings这一句则是形容前面的butterflies。 「（room） that was prepared at the palace at Rheims for the use of Queen Joan of Burgundy 」：在兰斯宫内为勃艮第的琼王后准备的（房间）。这里的be prepared for是“为···准备”， 「 was decorated with 」：以···装饰 整句话学了三个表示装饰的词语，大家都可以记一下哦 and blazoned with the king's arms, and five hundred and sixty-one butterflies, whose wings were similarly ornamented with the arms of the queen, the whole worked in gold." 「and blazoned with the king's arms」：以国王的徽章标记。这里的be blazoned with也是装饰的意思，而arm并不是手臂，而是徽章、徽记。 「were similarly ornamented with」：同样也装饰有···。
                            道林还读到为勃艮第的琼王后准备的兰斯王宫的内室， "装饰了一千三百二十只鹦鹉，身上都绘有国王的徽记，以及五百六十一只蝴蝶，每只蝴蝶的翅膀上都绘了皇后的徽记，鹦鹉和蝴蝶都是用金线绣成的。 "

                              【14】 Catherine de Medicis had a mourning-bed made for her of black velvet powdered with crescents（月亮） and suns. Its curtains were of damask, with leafy wreaths and garlands, figured upon a gold and silver ground, and fringed along the edges with broideries of pearls, 「figured upon」：用···描述 「fringed along the edges with broideries of pearls」：沿着边沿绣上珍珠作为流苏。Fringe with是“用···做流苏” 这里说的是上文提到的灵床，是不是有点恐怖呀。这里的it都是指的灵床。 灵床的幔子是锦缎做的，缀着叶圈（leafy wreaths）和花冠（garlands），用金银衬的底，边沿的流苏上绣的是珍珠。这张灵床安放在挂了一排排皇后的纹章的房间里，纹章是用剪碎的黑丝绒点缀在银布做成的。 and it stood in a room hung with rows of the queen's devices in cut black velvet upon cloth of silver. 「hung with rows of the queen's devices in cut black velvet upon cloth of silver」：挂着一排排皇后的纹章，纹章是剪碎的黑色天鹅绒缀在银布上做成的。这里的devices是纹章的意思，前面已经讲解过。Cut black velvet中的“cut”是过去分词做形容词，表现剪碎的。
                              卡特林．德．梅迪西让人为她准备的灵床，铺着饰有无数新月和太阳的黑丝绒。灵床的帐幔是锦缎做的，缀着叶圈和花冠，用金银衬的底，边沿的流苏上绣的是珍珠。这张灵床安放在挂了一排排皇后的纹章的房间里，纹章是用剪碎的黑丝绒点缀在银线织成的缎子匕做成的。

                                【15】 Louis XIV had gold embroidered caryatides（刻像柱子） fifteen feet high in his apartment. The state bed of Sobieski, King of Poland, was made of Smyrna gold brocade embroidered in turquoises with verses from the Koran. Its supports were of silver gilt, beautifully chased, and profusely set with enamelled and jewelled medallions. It had been taken from the Turkish camp before Vienna, and the standard of Mohammed had stood beneath the tremulous gilt of its canopy（华盖）.
                                路易十四的寓所里竖着一根高达十五英尺的镂金女子刻像柱子。波兰国王索别斯基的御用寝床料子是金线锦缎，装点着刻有古兰经文的绿松石。床柱是银做的，精雕细刻，嵌满了珐琅和宝石圆饰。这张床是在维也纳城前土耳其营帐中夺得的，当年穆罕默德的军旗曾悬挂在飘动的涂金华盖下。

                                  【16】 And so, for a whole year, he sought to accumulate the most exquisite specimens that he could find of textile and embroidered work, getting the dainty Delhi（德里） muslins, 「getting the dainty Delhi（德里） muslins」：得到精美的德里薄纱。这一成分是现在分词做非谓语。 finely wrought with gold-thread palmates and stitched over with iridescent beetles' wings; the Dacca gauzes（薄纱）, 「finely wrought with gold-thread palmates and stitched over with iridescent beetles' wings」：这里的wrought和stitched over都是过去分词做非谓语，finely则表示细致地。 that from their transparency are known in the East as "woven air," and "running water," and "evening dew";
                                  于是整整一年，道林力尽所能地收集着最珍贵的纺织和刺绣创样品，有精 美的德里薄纱织物，缀着金线织成的叶子和闪光的甲虫翅膀；有达卡的细罗，因其透明在东方被称之为"空气织品"、 "流水"乘"夜露"；

In the long cedar chests that lined the west gallery of his house, he had stored away many rare and beautiful specimens of what is really the raiment of the Bride of Christ, 这里的主句就是“he had stored away many rare and beautiful specimens of what is really the raiment of the Bride of Christ”，而后面则嵌套了好几个从句，我们来一一分析吧。 who must wear purple and jewels and fine linen that she may hide the pallid macerated body that is worn by the suffering that she seeks for and wounded by self-inflicted pain. 「 who must wear purple and jewels and fine linen」：这个从句是形容前面的the Bride of Christ。一般来说，基督的新妇主要指的“教会”，因此这里指的也是神职人员或者基督徒，历史上基督徒也遭受过很多迫害。 「 that she may hide the pallid macerated body」：这一从句是强调前面的珠宝、紫衣的作用。 「 that is worn by the suffering」：这一从句形容的是body，be worn by则是表示“因···受损”。 「 that she seeks for and wounded by self-inflicted pain」：这一从句形容的是前面的suffering。Seek for表示“寻找”，而self-inflicted pain是指“自找的痛苦”

                                          Notes

                                          1. Colosseum罗马斗兽场

                                          建于72-80年间，是古罗马文明的象征。遗址位于意大利首都罗马市中心，它在威尼斯广场的南面，古罗马市场附近。从外观上看，它呈正圆形；俯瞰时，它是椭圆形的。这座庞大的建筑可以容纳近九万人数的观众。

                                          罗马中心壮观的斗兽场是古罗马当时为取悦凯旋而归的将领士兵和赞美伟大的古罗马帝国而建造的。斗兽场的建筑设计并不落后于现代的美学观点，而事实上，大约2000年后的今天，每一个现代化的大型体育场都或多或少的烙上了一些古罗马斗兽场的设计风格。


                                          2. the Koran 古兰经

                                          《古兰经》是伊斯兰教的经典。伊斯兰教相信《古兰经》的原文在世界未创造之先早已存在天国，后由天使加百列(Gabriel) 启示穆罕默德才传至世间。穆罕默德从第一次得到启示（穆罕默德被安拉委为先知）到逝世的二十二年两个月又二十二天中，神的“教诲”逐节逐章不断地赐下来，由他口传给门徒。

                                          穆罕默德凭着《古兰经》的力量，把一盘散沙、四分五裂的阿拉伯民族联结成一个团结的民族，并使它的影响扩大到亚非欧广大地区的信教人民群众中去，使信教的各民族迅速接受了《古兰经》的经义和思想。《古兰经》作为伊斯兰教的圣典而存于世，并且有极大的影响力。


                                          熟词生义

                                          1. 「certain」：这个词语很多时候表示确定的，但是在本章中的意思是“某种”，

                                          原文：Leonardus Camillus had seen a white stone taken from the brain of a newly killed toad, that was a certain antidote against poison.

                                          e.g.  The summer heat withered up certain plants. 盛夏炎热使某些植物萎缩了。

                                          2. 「Arms」：在本章中并不是表示我们常见的“手臂”的意思哦，而是表示“纹章，徽章”。

                                          原文：whose wings were similarly ornamented with the arms of the queen.

                                          e.g. The red rose is our family arm.红色的玫瑰使我们的家族徽章。

                                          3. 「faculty 」：这个词语一般指学院或者科、系，但是本章使用的是“能力”的意思，即one of the inherent cognitive or perceptual powers of the mind。

                                          原文： and he always had an extraordinary faculty of becoming absolutely absorbed for the moment in whatever he took up.

                                          E. g. Imagination is your most powerful faculty.  想象力是你最强大的能力。

                                          4. 「device」：在本章中这个词语不是指设备哦，而是指图案、图纹， 即any ornamental pattern or design as in embroidery.

                                          原文：and it stood in a room hung with rows of the queen's devices in cut black velvet upon cloth of silver.

                                          E.g. He was recognized by the device on his shield. 他因为盾牌上的图案被认了出来。


                                          今日短语

                                          1. Wax and wane 兴衰

                                          2. Be decorated with 装饰

                                          3. Take up 着手做某事


                                          句子解析

                                          1. The selenite（石膏石） waxed and waned with the moon, and the meloceus（宝石名）, that discovers thieves, could be affected only by the blood of kids.

                                          「waxed and waned」：阴晴圆缺。Wax and wane有“兴衰、盈亏”的意思，因此这里是表示月亮的阴晴圆缺。

                                          「 be affected only by the blood of kids」：小山羊的血会使其失效。“be affected by”是“受···影响”，而这里的kids并不是指小孩，而是指小山羊。

                                          句子大意：石膏石会随月亮的盈亏而增减；一种叫做“梅洛西亚斯”的宝石能识别窃贼，只有小山羊的血会使其失效。


                                          2. The gates of the palace of John the Priest were "made of sardius（红宝石）, with the horn of the horned snake inwrought（镶嵌）, so that no man might bring poison within."

                                          「with the horn of the horned snake inwrought（镶嵌）」：镶嵌着角蛇的角。这一短语中第一个horn是名词，第二个horn是动词，表示“有角的”，而inwrought是后置定语，表示“嵌入的”

                                          「so that no man might bring poison within」：所以没有人可以把毒药带进来。Bring within是“把···带进来”的意思。

                                          句子大意：牧师约翰的官门是“用红宝石做成的，镶嵌着角蛇的角，使携毒者不得进入”。


                                          3. Over the gable were "two golden apples, in which were two carbuncles（红宝石）," so that the gold might shine by day and the carbuncles by night.

                                          「Over the gable 」：山墙之上

                                          「in which were two carbuncles（红宝石）」：内有两颗红宝石。这一篇中出现了大量的珠宝器物的名字，小伙伴们是不是晕了呢~

                                          「so that the gold might shine by day and the carbuncles by night」：金子在白天闪光，红玉在夜间发亮。这句话的and后面省略了shine，可以补充上去。

                                          句子大意：山墙之上放着“两个金苹果，内有两块红玉”，金子在白天闪光，红玉在夜间发亮。


                                          4. In Lodge's strange romance 'A Margarite of America', it was stated that in the chamber of the queen one could behold（看见） "all the chaste ladies of the world, inchased out of silver, looking through fair mirrours of chrysolites（橄榄石）, carbuncles（红宝石）, sapphires（蓝宝石）, and greene emeraults（绿宝石）.

                                          「 strange romance」：怪异的传奇小说

                                          「 it was stated 」：据说

                                          「inchased out of silver」：银刻的。这里的inchased表示雕刻，是过去分词做形容词的用法，形容前的ladies。

                                          「 looking through fair mirrours of ···」：从···镜中显露出来。Look throgh这里是表示“显露出来”。

                                          句子大意：洛奇的一部怪异的传奇《美洲的一颗珍珠》提到，在皇后的寝宫里可以看见“世间所有贞洁女子的银镂刻像，从橄榄石、红玉、蓝宝石和绿宝石的镜子里显露出来”。

                                          这里出现的宝石可真多啊，小伙伴千万不要被吓到，只要了解一下就好啦


                                          5. A sea-monster had been enamoured of the pearl that the diver brought to King Perozes, and had slain the thief, and mourned for seven moons over its loss.

                                          「had been enamoured of 」：迷恋

                                          「 mourned for seven moons over its loss」：哀悼了七个月。Moon也不仅仅指月亮，可以用来指月份。

                                          句子大意：一个海怪迷上了被潜水员取来献给国王皮罗萨斯的一颗珍珠，杀死了窃珠人，并为自己的损失哀悼了七个月。


                                          6. When the Huns（匈奴人） lured the king into the great pit, he flung it away— Procopius tells the story—nor was it ever found again, though the Emperor Anastasius offered five hundred-weight of gold pieces for it.

                                          「 lured the king into the great pit」：把国王引诱进陷阱。

                                          「— Procopius tells the story—」：这是一句插入语，往往表示某人说。

                                          「nor was it ever found again」：再也没有找到（这颗珍珠）。这里是一个半倒装的句子，nor后面的be动词提前到主语之前。还原成正常的语序是“it was not ever found again”。小伙伴们可能还要问，为什么要用nor呢？这主要取决于王尔德写作时的目的，可能他想通过使用nor来加强与前句的联系。

                                          「 five hundred-weight of gold pieces for it」：为之付出五百单位的黄金。Weight除了表示重量，也可以表示砝码。这里文章并没有明示这个砝码的重量为多少，因此我们理解为“单位”。其实最好的方式，就是理解为很多很多黄金啦~

                                          句子大意：后来匈奴人把国王诱入陷阱时，据普罗科皮埃斯说，国王扔掉了珍珠。尽管阿那斯塔希斯国王出了相当于五百单位的悬赏。


                                          7. Hall described Henry VIII, on his way to the Tower previous to his coronation, as wearing "a jacket of raised gold, the placard embroidered with diamonds and other rich stones, and a great bauderike（饰品） about his neck of large balasses（玫瑰红尖晶石）.

                                          「on his way to」：在去···的路上

                                          「a jacket of raised gold」：凸花纹金线上衣。这里的raised指的是凸起的纹路，而gold是指金丝线。

                                          「 and a great bauderike（饰品） about his neck of large balasses」：脖子上有一块大饰品，由大块的玫瑰红尖晶石制成。

                                          句子大意：霍尔描写亨利八世在加冕前去伦敦塔的路上，身穿“凸花纹金丝线上衣”，胸牌上饰有钻石和其他宝石，颈项上有一大块饰品，缀有巨大的玫瑰红尖晶石。


                                          8. As he investigated the subject— and he always had an extraordinary faculty of becoming absolutely absorbed for the moment in whatever he took up—he was almost saddened by the reflection of the ruin that time brought on beautiful and wonderful things.

                                          「 had an extraordinary faculty of」：拥有特殊的能力。

                                          「 becoming absolutely absorbed for the moment in whatever he took up」：变得极度专注于着手的东西。become absorbed in表示专注于。Take up是表示“着手某事”

                                          「was almost saddened by」：为···感到悲哀

                                          「the reflection of the ruin that time brought on beautiful and wonderful things」：关于时间带给美妙事物的摧残的想法。The reflection of表示“关于···的想法”，that后面的句子是ruin的定语从句，还原成正常语序为“time brought ruin on beaufiful and wonderful things”。

                                          句子大意：当他钻研这个目标，他总有一种特殊的能力，会一时间极度专注于着手的东西--便几乎为这个目标所带来的想法而感到悲哀，即时间总是给美妙的事物带来摧残。


                                          9. Summer followed summer, and the yellow jonquils（黄水仙） bloomed and died many times, and nights of horror repeated the story of their shame, but he was unchanged. No winter marred his face or stained his flowerlike bloom.

                                          「Summer followed summer」：一个又一个夏天过去

                                          「and nights of horror repeated the story of their shame」：恐怖的夜，可耻的事情一次次发生。这里的nights被拟人化，好像在陈述可耻的故事。

                                          「No winter marred his face or stained his flowerlike bloom」：冬天无法损毁他的脸或他花朵般的青春。

                                          句子大意：一个夏天又一个夏天过去了，黄色的长寿花开了又谢。恐怖的夜晚，那些可耻的事情一次次发生，而他自己却依然未变。冬天没有损毁他的容颜，或者玷污他花朵般的青春。


                                          10. Where the huge velarium（天幕） that Nero had stretched across the Colosseum（罗马斗兽场） at Rome, that Titan sail of purple on which was represented the starry sky, and Apollo driving a chariot （二轮战车）drawn by white, gilt-reined steeds?

                                          这句话的主干是Where was the huge velarium，只是省略了was一次。王尔德这么写有一种诗歌的味道。后面是and连接的两个成分，分别是“that Nero had”和“that Titan sail···”。第二个成分里又嵌套了一个从句“on which was····”

                                          「that Nero had stretched across the Colosseum（罗马斗兽场） at Rome」：尼禄要铺盖在罗马剧场的那块巨大的（天幕）。这句话是velarium的定语从句。Stretch across是盖在···上面。

                                          「that Titan sail of purple on which was represented the starry sky」：巨型的紫色风帆，上面画着星光闪耀的天空。Titan是“巨大的”意思，来源于古希腊神话中的泰坦巨神。On which从句的语序可以还原为“the starry sky was represented on which”，而which指的是sail。

                                          「Apollo driving a chariot （二轮战车）drawn by white, gilt-reined steeds」：阿波罗驾驶着二轮战车，由两匹马驱使。Steed指的是战马。这一成分与前面的starry sky并列，并不是一个完整的句子。

                                          句子大意：尼禄要铺在罗马剧场的那块巨大的天幕，那张巨型紫色风帆，上面画着星光闪耀的天空和阿波罗驾着白骏马配镀金缰绳的战场，如今又在哪里呢？

                                          11. He longed to see the curious table-napkins wrought for the Priest of the Sun, on which were displayed all the dainties（美食） and viands（佳肴） that could be wanted for a feast;

                                          「longed to 」：渴望

                                          「wrought for the Priest of the Sun」：为太阳神祭司所作的··。Wrought是work的过去分词，表示“为···精制”

                                          「on which were displayed all the dainties（美食） and viands（佳肴）」：美味佳肴全描绘在上面。这个定语从句可以还原成“dainties and viands were displayed on which”，而这的which指的是前面的napkins，

                                          句子大意：道林渴望见到那些为太阳神祭司编织的奇异餐巾，上面绣有盛宴所需的一切美食和佳肴。


                                          12. and the coat that Charles of Orleans once wore, on the sleeves of which were embroidered the verses of a song beginning "Madame, je suis tout joyeux," the musical accompaniment of the words being wrought in gold thread, and each note, of square shape in those days, formed with four pearls.

                                          这里并没有完整的句子主干，而是短语，只是短语后面有一些从句或分词结构。这里的外套就是道林向要看到的东西之一。

                                          句子大意：还有奥尔良的查理穿过的外套，袖子上绣着一首歌，起句是“夫人，我非常高兴”，配乐的歌词是用金线绣成的，当年画成方形的每个音符由四颗珍珠来代表。

                                          「on the sleeves of which were embroidered the verses of a song」：袖子上绣着一首歌。这句话可以还原成the verses of a song were embroidered on the sleeves of which，而which指的是the coat。

                                          「the musical accompaniment of the words」：歌词的音乐伴奏。accompaniment是伴奏的意思。

                                          「and each note, of square shape in those days, formed with four pearls」：这是完整的一句，只是用逗号分开了。这里的note指的是“音符”，这些音符在过去是画成方形的。


                                          13. He read of the room /that was prepared at the palace at Rheims for the use of Queen Joan of Burgundy and was decorated with "thirteen hundred and twenty-one parrots, made in broidery（刺绣）, and blazoned with the king's arms, and five hundred and sixty-one butterflies,/ whose wings were similarly ornamented with the arms of the queen, the whole worked in gold.

                                          这句话的主干就是“He read of the room”，后面的成分都是修饰的成分。Room后面跟了好几个并列的句子，分别是“was prepared at”，“was decorated with”，“and blazoned with”。Whose wings这一句则是形容前面的butterflies。

                                          「（room） that was prepared at the palace at Rheims for the use of Queen Joan of Burgundy 」：在兰斯宫内为勃艮第的琼王后准备的（房间）。这里的be prepared for是“为···准备”，

                                          「 was decorated with 」：以···装饰

                                          「and blazoned with the king's arms」：以国王的徽章标记。这里的be blazoned with也是装饰的意思，而arm并不是手臂，而是徽章、徽记。

                                          「were similarly ornamented with」：同样也装饰有···。

                                          这里学了三个表示装饰的词语，大家都可以记一下哦，


                                          14. Its curtains were of damask, with leafy wreaths and garlands, figured upon a gold and silver ground, and fringed along the edges with broideries of pearls, and it stood in a room hung with rows of the queen's devices in cut black velvet upon cloth of silver.

                                          这里说的是上文提到的灵床，是不是有点恐怖呀。这里的it都是指的灵床。

                                          灵床的幔子是锦缎做的，缀着叶圈（leafy wreaths）和花冠（garlands），用金银衬的底，边沿的流苏上绣的是珍珠。这张灵床安放在挂了一排排皇后的纹章的房间里，纹章是用剪碎的黑丝绒点缀在银布做成的。

                                          「figured upon」：用···描述

                                          「fringed along the edges with broideries of pearls」：沿着边沿绣上珍珠作为流苏。Fringe with是“用···做流苏”

                                          「hung with rows of the queen's devices in cut black velvet upon cloth of silver」：挂着一排排皇后的纹章，纹章是剪碎的黑色天鹅绒缀在银布上做成的。这里的devices是纹章的意思，前面已经讲解过。Cut black velvet中的“cut”是过去分词做形容词，表现剪碎的。


                                          15. And so, for a whole year, he sought to accumulate the most exquisite specimens that he could find of textile and embroidered work, getting the dainty Delhi（德里） muslins, finely wrought with gold-thread palmates and stitched over with iridescent beetles' wings;

                                          于是整整一年，道林力尽所能地收集这最珍贵的纺织（textile）和刺绣样品（embroidered work），有精美的德里薄纱，缀着金线织成的叶子（gold-thread palmates）和闪光的甲虫翅膀（iridescent beetles’ wings）。

                                          「getting the dainty Delhi（德里） muslins」：得到精美的德里薄纱。这一成分是现在分词做非谓语。

                                          「finely wrought with gold-thread palmates and stitched over with iridescent beetles' wings」：这里的wrought和stitched over都是过去分词做非谓语，finely则表示细致地。


                                          16. In the long cedar chests that lined the west gallery of his house, he had stored away many rare and beautiful specimens of what is really the raiment of the Bride of Christ, who must wear purple and jewels and fine linen that she may hide the pallid macerated body that is worn by the suffering that she seeks for and wounded by self-inflicted pain.

                                          一排长长的杉木柜子排列在（lined）房子西廊，那里藏着（stored away）许多珍稀而漂亮的样品，是属于基督新妇的衣服，她得穿紫色的衣袍和精制的麻布，戴上珠宝，才能藏住自找的苦难所造成的苍白消瘦（pallid macerated）的身体，

                                          这里的主句就是“he had stored away many rare and beautiful specimens of what is really the raiment of the Bride of Christ”，而后面则嵌套了好几个从句，我们来一一分析吧。

                                          「 who must wear purple and jewels and fine linen」：这个从句是形容前面的the Bride of Christ。一般来说，基督的新妇主要指的“教会”，因此这里指的也是神职人员或者基督徒，历史上基督徒也遭受过很多迫害。

                                          「 that she may hide the pallid macerated body」：这一从句是强调前面的珠宝、紫衣的作用。

                                          「 that is worn by the suffering」：这一从句形容的是body，be worn by则是表示“因···受损”。

                                          「 that she seeks for and wounded by self-inflicted pain」：这一从句形容的是前面的suffering。Seek for表示“寻找”，而self-inflicted pain是指“自找的痛苦”


                                          17. He possessed a gorgeous cope of crimson silk and gold-thread damask, /figured with a repeating pattern of golden pomegranates set in six-petalled formal blossoms, /beyond which on either side was the pine-apple device wrought in seed-pearls.

                                          这里的主干是“He possessed a gorgeous cope of crimson silk and gold-thread damask”，“figureed with···”是分词引导的非谓语结构，而后面则是一个定语从句。

                                          道林有一件用深红色的丝线和金线锦缎（gold-thread）做成的华丽法衣（a gorgeous cope），金色的石榴图案依次（repeating）镶嵌(set in)在六瓣式的花瓣上，其上端的两侧（beyond which on either side）是细珍珠组成的凤梨图案。

                                          「figured with」：描绘着，完整形式应该是be figured with

                                          「 on either side」：在任意两侧

                                          这么多长长的句子，是不是很难呢？其实这些句子的结构并不复杂，主要是形容词、名词非常高级，所以小伙伴们才感到头晕目眩，可以再仔细看看哦。
