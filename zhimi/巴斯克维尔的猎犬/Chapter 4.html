                                              熟词生义

                                              1. 「line」：我们最熟悉的意思应该是”线条；（有人排队的）队伍”了吧，今天这个意思大家应该很陌生，是作为“家系；家族”，即a series of people, things or events that follow one another in time，是在说Baskerville这一家族。

                                              原文：In these days of nouveaux riches（暴发户） it is refreshing to find a case where the scion（子孙） of an old county family which has fallen upon evil days is able to make his own fortune and to bring it back with him to restore the fallen grandeur of his line.

                                              e.g. She came from a long line of doctors.她来自一个医生世家。


                                              2. 「speculation」我们一般常用到它的动词形式“speculate”，常做“沉思”义，所以名词形式会很容易想到“沉思”，但是它还有一个意思是“投机买卖”哦，动词名词都有这个意思，the activity of buying and selling goods or shares in a company in the hope of making a profit, but with the risk of losing mone，经常阅读财经相关外刊的应该不陌生吧~

                                              原文：Sir Charles, as is well known, made large sums of money in South African speculation.

                                              e.g. The government has raised interest rates in an attempt to protect its currency from speculation. 政府提高了利率以防止货币投机买卖。


                                              3. 「but」：又是这个“but”，目前我们已经知道它“（表转折）但是”，还有“只是（相当于only）”这两个用法了，那今天又是什么意思呢？今天是作介词表示“除了”，其实“除了”这个意思我们应该经常见到“nothing but.......”这个里面的but就是“除了”，但是今天可能会有很多同学辨别不出来哦，是说大众把死因归之各种原因，除了自然的原因。

                                              原文：There is no reason whatever to suspect foul play（谋杀）, or to imagine that death could be from any but natural causes.

                                              e.g. The problem is anything but easy.这个问题一点也不简单。


                                              4. 「affection」：今天文中可不是表达“喜爱”了哦，而是和“喜爱”还有点反差的意思，是“疾病；病情”，是在说Sir Charles有心脏方面的疾病。

                                              原文：Their evidence, corroborated by that of several friends, tends to show that Sir Charles’s health has for some time been impaired, and points especially to some affection of the heart......

                                              e.g. The affection and clinical changes were observed. 分别观察镇痛作用效果及临床病情变化

                                              3. take up 占领，占据，占有(位置、阵地)

                                              4. clear up 解释

                                              5. give rise to 造成；引起；导致

                                              6. dispose of 解决；驳倒

                                              7. consist of 包括；由…组成

                                              8. be in the habit of 习惯于…

                                              9. in search of 追寻；搜索

                                              10. be borne out 被证实

                                              11. in accordance with 与…一致

                                              12. shrink from 因害怕而避开

                                              13. result from 产生于…； 由…引起


                                              句子解析

                                              1. In these days of nouveaux riches（暴发户） it is refreshing to find a case where the scion（子孙） of an old county family which has fallen upon evil days is able to make his own fortune and to bring it back with him to restore the fallen grandeur of his line.

                                              「In these days of nouveaux riches」：至此暴发户充斥之际

                                              「which has fallen upon evil days」：修饰这个old county family，county family，世代居于一郡的望族。这一名门望族曾在厄运的日子没落了

                                              「make his own fortune」：自己能发家致富

                                              「restore the fallen grandeur of his line」：重建家族的辉煌


                                              连起来大意就是：值此暴发户充斥之时，如Sir Charles这样一位名门之后，竟能致富还乡，重振因厄运而中衰之家族，实为可喜之事。

                                              大家有没有想过这个“evil days（这段充满厄运的日子）”是指啥，会使得家道中落？其实就是我们昨天阅读中接触到的那位Hugo Baskerville的离奇死亡之后开始的，自此家中无人敢深夜踏足沼泽地，郡上应该也是谣言四起


                                              2. Sir Charles, as is well known, made large sums of money in South African speculation. More wise than those who go on until the wheel turns against them, he realized his gains and returned to England with them.

                                              「speculation」：这个词我们熟词生义说过了哦，是“投机买卖”，Sir Charles靠着在南非的投机买卖大赚了一笔

                                              「those who go on until the wheel turns against them」：那些没有停止，直到轮子倒转向他们自己的人。其实也就是说那些没有适可而止结果赔了的人

                                              「realized his gains」：realize这里是“使......变现”哦，就是说Sir Charles把得到的资产变现


                                              整句话的意思是：众所周知，Sir Charles曾在南非投资致富。但他选择全身而退，比那些干到倒霉的人聪明，携带变卖了的资财返回了英伦。


                                              3. The circumstances connected with the death of Sir Charles cannot be said to have been entirely cleared up by the inquest（验尸）, but at least enough has been done to dispose of those rumours to which local superstition（迷信） has given rise.

                                              「The circumstances connected with the death of Sir Charles」：关于Sir Charles之死的情况。

                                              「cleared up」：解释。这里是说并没有完全因验尸而被解释清楚。

                                              「dispose of those rumours」：驳倒那些流言

                                              「to which local superstition has given rise」：先行词是rumours，修饰它，试着将先行词代入用正常语序看这个句子：local superstition has given rise to those rumours(which)，当地的迷信所引起的那些流言。


                                              那么这整个句子的意思就是：验尸结果尚未足以将Sir Charles死亡的相关情况厘清，至少足以驳倒当地由于迷信所引起的诸多谣言。


                                              4. Their evidence, corroborated by that of several friends, tends to show that Sir Charles’s health has for some time been impaired, and points especially to some affection of the heart, manifesting itself in changes of colour, breathlessness, and acute attacks of nervous depression.

                                              这句抓一抓主干，“Their evidence...tends to show that...”，他们的证据显示...

                                              「corroborated by that of several friends」：修饰的是“Their evidence”，这些证据，已被Sir Charles几个朋友证实的证据。

                                              「points especially to some affection of the heart」：与心脏疾病有关

                                              「manifesting itself in」：表现为...


                                              整句的意思就是：他们的证词——已被几个朋友的陈述所证实——说明，Sir Charles曾一度有健康状况不良之征兆，尤其是在心脏官能方面，表现为面色改变、呼吸困难和严重的神经衰弱。


                                              5. One fact which has not been explained is the statement of Barrymore that his master’s footprints altered their character from the time that he passed the moor-gate, and that he appeared from thence onward to have been walking upon his toes.

                                              还是先抓一抓主干，“One fact...is the statement...”，有一件事实是...

                                              「which has not been explained」：修饰fact，有一个还未被解释清楚的事实。

                                              「altered their character」：改变了样子

                                              「walking upon his toes」：用脚尖走路，就是踮着脚走。


                                              这句意思就是：有一件尚未得到解释的事实是：Barrymore说，他主人的足迹在过了通往沼地的旁门后就变了样，好像是从那以后就改用足尖走路了。


                                              6. No signs of violence were to be discovered upon Sir Charles’s person, and though the doctor’s evidence pointed to an almost incredible facial distortion—so great that Dr. Mortimer refused at first to believe that it was indeed his friend and patient who lay before him—it was explained that that is a symptom which is not unusual in cases of dyspnoea（呼吸困难） and death from cardiac exhaustion（心脏衰竭）.

                                              「an almost incredible facial distortion」：几乎令人难以置信的面部扭曲

                                              「so great that Dr. Mortimer refused at first to believe」：又是“so...that...”，“如此...以至于...”的一个结构，如此大的扭曲，以至于Dr. Mortimer刚开始拒绝相信。

                                              「which is not unusual in cases of dyspnoea and death from cardiac exhaustion」：not unusual，双重否定表肯定，那就是is usual，这在因呼吸困难和心脏衰竭而死的案例中很常见。


                                              连起来大意就是：在Sir Charles身上找不到遭受暴力袭击的痕迹，可是医生的证明中指出，死者的面容严重变形，以致于一开始Dr. Mortimer都难以相信，那躺在面前的就是他的朋友和病人的尸体——据解释说，这是一种在因呼吸困难和心脏衰竭而死的时候常会出现的现象。


                                              7. It is well that this is so, for it is obviously of the utmost importance that Sir Charles’s heir should settle at the Hall and continue the good work which has been so sadly interrupted.

                                              「It is well that this is so」：事件如此结束尚属妥善

                                              「for it is obviously of the utmost importance」：“for”作连词，表原因，be of the utmost importance，又是“be+of+抽象名词”的用法，相当于抽象名词的形容词义。因为显然最重要的是...


                                              大意就是：事件如此结束尚属妥善，因Sir Charles的后代仍将在庄园居住，并将继续这一不幸中断的善行。


                                              8. Had the prosaic（平庸的）finding of the coroner not finally put an end to the romantic stories which have been whispered in connection with the affair, it might have been difficult to find a tenant for Baskerville Hall.

                                              虚拟语气的倒装结构，出现了无数次了，大家应该能准确判断了吧~还是来复习一下吧~

                                              虚拟条件句的从句部分如果含有were, should, 或had, 可将if省略,再把were, should或had 移到从句句首,实行倒装，主句仍保持正常语序。

                                              那么把这个句子的条件句用正常的虚拟语气语序表达看一下：If the prosaic finding of the coroner had not finally put an end to the romantic stories...，如果验尸官平庸的调查未能最终平息那些虚构的故事。


                                              所以这个完整的意思就是：如果验尸官平庸的调查不能最终平息那些谣言，就恐怕很难再为巴斯克维尔庄园找到住户了。

                                              当然咯，庄园内会有恶犬索命，谁敢往里面住呢？

                                              (透着一丝恐怖的巴斯克维尔庄园）


                                              9. It is understood that the next of kin is Mr. Henry Baskerville, if he be still alive, the son of Sir Charles Baskerville’s younger brother. The young man when last heard of was in America, and inquiries are being instituted with a view to informing him of his good fortune.

                                              「the next of kin」：最近的血亲

                                              「with a view to informing him of his good fortune」：with a view to，为了，目的在于...，举个栗子：
                                              e.g. The authors no doubt overstated their case with a view to catching the public's attention.  这些作者无疑夸大了实情以吸引公众的注意力。

                                              inform sb. of sth.，把...通知某人，举个栗子：

                                              e.g. Please inform us of any changes of address. 地址若有变动请随时通知我们。


                                              那么这个句子就是说：据了解，Sir Charles现存最近的血亲是他弟弟的儿子Mr. Henry Baskerville，如果这位仁兄还活着的话。最后一次得到的消息的时候是听说这位年轻人在美洲。目前已着手进行调查，以便通知他来接受这笔为数庞大的财产。


                                              10. My motive for withholding it from the coroner’s inquiry is that a man of science shrinks from placing himself in the public position of seeming to indorse（认可） a popular superstition.

                                              抓抓句子主干“My motive...is that...”，我的动机是这样的...

                                              「shrink from」：因害怕而避开...。在看个例子吧：

                                              e.g. I do not shrink from this responsibility-I welcome it.

                                              我决不在这责任之前退缩；我欢迎它。

                                              「placing himself in the public position of seeming to indorse a popular superstition」：placing himself in the public position，将他自己置于公众面前，of seeming to indorse a popular superstition，显得似乎认可这种广为流传的迷信说法。


                                              完整的句子大意就是：因为一个从事科学工作的人，最怕在公众面前显示出他似乎也相信了一种流传的迷信。
