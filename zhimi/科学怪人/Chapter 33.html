【0】 "I remember, the first time I did this, the young woman, when she opened the door in the morning, appeared greatly astonished on seeing such a great pile of wood on the outside.
“我记得我第一次帮他们砍完柴的第二天早上，那个姑娘打开门，看见门口放着一大堆柴火，简直大吃一惊。

    【1】 She uttered some words in a loud voice, and the youth joined her, who also expressed surprise. I observed, with pleasure, that he did not go to the forest that day, but spent it in repairing the cottage and cultivating the garden.
    她忍不住大声嚷嚷起来，闻声出来的小伙子一看也惊得目瞪口呆。我很高兴小伙子那天没有到林子里去，而是忙着修理房子，或者在菜园里耕地施肥。”

      【2】 "By degrees I made a discovery of still greater a moment. I found that these people possessed a method of communicating their experience and feelings to one another by articulate sounds. I perceived that the words they spoke sometimes produced pleasure or pain, smiles or sadness, in the minds and countenances of the hearers.
      　　“接着，我又有了更重要的发现。我发现他们有一种方法，可以通过发出清晰的音节来交流彼此的经历和情感。我注意到他们说的话，能让听者时而高兴，时而难过，而他们的面部表情也会随着时阴时情。

        【3】 This was indeed a godlike science, and I ardently desired to become acquainted with it. But I was baffled in every attempt I made for this purpose. Their pronunciation was quick, and the words they uttered, not having any apparent connection with visible objects, I was unable to discover any clue by which I could unravel the mystery of their reference. 「have connection with」：与……有联系 「unravel」：解开 「reference」：本意为“谈到、涉及（的事物）”，在文中就是指他们谈话的内容。 这句话描写的是“我”学习人类语言的过程中发现的难点。大家也都知道，学语言是一个循序渐进的过程，我们小时候刚学英语的时候也是从ABC开始学，但是文中的怪物一开始接触的就是日常对话啦，实在是太难为他了。但是即便如此，他也是不会轻易放弃滴。
        这个本领真是太神奇了，我迫切地想学会这种方法。可是我绞尽脑汁也白搭，他们吐字太快，而且所说的东西和眼前具体的实物没有明显的联系，所以我无法找到任何规律，也就不明白他们到底在说什么。”

          【4】 By great application, however, and after having remained during the space of several revolutions of the moon in my hovel, 「application」：这个词在文中不是“应用”的意思哦，而是“努力”的意思。 举个栗子： Success as a writer demands great application. 作家要成功就得悉力以赴。 「revolutions of the moon」：月亮的阴晴圆缺，也就是说过了几个月以后。 「hovel」：（不适宜居住的）小屋 I discovered the names that were given to some of the most familiar objects of discourse; I learned and applied the words, fire, milk, bread, and wood. 「discourse」：谈话 经过不懈的努力，怪物终于掌握了一些常用的单词。
          　　“不过，通过我的努力，在经过月亮的几次阴晴圆缺之后，我终于明白了一些我最熟悉的东西的名称，比如我学会了‘火’、‘牛奶’、‘面包’和‘柴火’等等。

            【5】 I learned the names of the cottagers themselves. The youth and his companion had each of them several names, but the old man had only one, which was father. The girl was called sister or Agatha, and the youth Felix, brother, or son.
            我还弄清楚了这家人的名字，小伙子和姑娘每人都有好几个称呼，但是那个老人却只有一个，叫做‘父亲’.他们管那姑娘叫‘妹妹’或者‘阿加莎’，管小伙子叫‘费利克斯’、‘哥哥’或者‘儿子’。

              【6】 I cannot describe the delight I felt when I learned the ideas appropriated to each of these sounds and was able to pronounce them. I distinguished several other words without being able as yet to understand or apply them, such as good, dearest, unhappy.
              当我弄明白这些词代表了什么意思，而且自己也能说得出来的时候，别提有多高兴了。我还可以分辨出另外一些词，例如‘好’、‘亲爱的’、‘不高兴’等等，但是我还不明白它们的意思，也不会运用。”

                【7】 "I spent the winter in this manner. The gentle manners and beauty of the cottagers greatly endeared them to me; when they were unhappy, I felt depressed; when they rejoiced, I sympathised in their joys.                   【8】 The old man, I could perceive, often endeavoured to encourage his children, as sometimes I found that he called to them, to cast off their melancholy. He would talk in a cheerful accent, and an expression of goodness that bestowed pleasure upon me.
                  我看出来那个老人经常给他的一双儿女打气鼓劲儿。因为，有的时候他会把他们叫到自己跟前，努力劝解他们。他说起话来那么和颜悦色，看了甚至让我都感到很舒心。

                    【9】 Agatha listened with respect, her eyes sometimes filled with tears, which she endeavoured to wipe away unperceived; but I generally found that her countenance and tone were more cheerful after having listened to the exhortations（开导） of her father.
                    阿加莎总是满怀敬意地听他说话，时不时还会热泪盈眶，但是她总是悄悄地把泪水抹去。在听完父亲的开导之后，阿加莎脸上的神色和说话的语气通常会变得开朗起来，

                      【10】 It was not thus with Felix. He was always the saddest of the group, and even to my unpractised senses, he appeared to have suffered more deeply than his friends. But if his countenance was more sorrowful, his voice was more cheerful than that of his sister, especially when he addressed the old man.
                      但是费利克斯不是这样。他是三个人当中最忧郁的，就连我这样迟钝的感觉都能察觉出他比另外两个人内心更痛苦。不过，虽然他的神色更悲伤，但是声音听上去却显的比他妹妹更轻松，尤其是他在和老人说话的时候。”

                        【11】 "I could mention innumerable instances which, although slight, marked the dispositions of these amiable cottagers. In the midst of poverty and want, Felix carried with pleasure to his sister the first little white flower that peeped out from beneath the snowy ground.
                        　　“有很多例子简直举不胜举，虽然都是一些不起眼儿的小事，但是却显示出这家人和蔼温和的气质。比如，尽管他们生活困窘、清贫如洗，但是费利克斯会兴高采烈地把从积雪覆盖着的大地上绽放出来的第一朵小白花摘下来，送给妹妹。

                          凌晨，当阿加莎还没有起床的时候，他会先把妹妹去挤奶时要经过的那条小路上的积雪打扫干净，然后再到井边打水，最后再从外屋去拿柴火——不过每次都让他非常惊讶，因为柴火每次都被一只他看不见的手堆得更高了。

                            【13】 In the day, I believe, he worked sometimes for a neighbouring farmer, because he often went forth and did not return until dinner, yet brought no wood with him.                             我想，他白天有时可能去邻近的农户去帮工，因为他去后常常要等到吃晚饭的时候才回家，而且也没有带柴火回来。其他的时间，他就在菜地里面干活。而眼下正是天寒地冻的时节，菜园里没什么活，那么他就会读书给老人和阿加莎听。”

                              【14】 "This reading had puzzled me extremely at first, but by degrees I discovered that he uttered many of the same sounds when he read as when he talked.                                   【16】 "I had admired the perfect forms of my cottagers-their grace, beauty, and delicate complexions; but how was I terrified when I viewed myself in a transparent pool!
                                  　　“我始终对邻居家的完美形象钦佩不已——他们风度翩翩，高贵优雅，俊美细腻。可是当我在清澈的池水里看到自己的倒影时，简直吓得倒抽冷气！

                                    【17】 At first I started back, unable to believe that it was indeed I who was reflected in the mirror; and when I became fully convinced that I was in reality the monster that I am, I was filled with the bitterest sensations of despondence（沮丧） and mortification（自卑）. Alas! I did not yet entirely know the fatal effects of this miserable deformity.

                                        【19】 Their food, as I afterwards found, was coarse, but it was wholesome; and they procured a sufficiency of it. Several new kinds of plants sprang up in the garden, which they dressed; and these signs of comfort increased daily as the season advanced.
                                        我后来发现，他们的食物虽然很粗劣，但是却很健康，而且供应充足。他们的菜园里面，长出了好几种新鲜的蔬菜；总之，随着春天临近，他们的境遇日渐好转。”

                                          【20】 "The old man, leaning on his son, walked each day at noon, when it did not rain, and I found it was called when the heavens poured forth its waters. 「lean on」：靠在…..上 「pour forth」：倾泻 最后“as I found…”这里，“it”指代的就是前面提到的“rain”，“我”发现人们会用到这个词，当天上有水倒下来的时候。 This frequently took place, but a high wind quickly dried the earth, and the season became far more pleasant than it had been.
                                          　　“只要天不下雨，老人每天都会扶着他的儿子在院子里散步。我知道‘下雨’是因为，每次天上有水倒下来时，他们就会说到这个词。天常常下雨，但是风也很急，很快就把大地吹干了。气候越来越宜人了。”

                                            Chapter 33 内容精讲

                                            熟词生义

                                            1. 「revolution」：“revolution”除了“革命”的意思之外，还有“天体运行”的意思。

                                            原文：After having remained during the space of several revolutions of the moon in my hovel, I discovered…

                                            e.g. the revolution of the earth around the sun

                                            地球环绕太阳的公转


                                            2. 「manner」：“manner”有“举止、礼仪”的意思，大家一定已经非常熟悉了。但是在文中是“方式、方法”的意思。

                                            原文：I spent the winter in this manner.  在这里“in this manner”就等于“in this way”。

                                            e.g. The manner in which the decision was announced was extremely regrettable.

                                            宣布决定的方式非常令人遗憾。


                                            3. 「address」：在之前的讲义中也有讲过“address”的熟词生义哦，不知道大家还记不记得呀。文中“address”不是“地址”的意思，而是“和……说话”的意思。

                                            原文： But if his countenance was more sorrowful, his voice was more cheerful than that of his sister, especially when he addressed the old man.

                                            e.g. I was surprised when he addressed me in English.

                                            他用英语跟我说话，我很诧异。


                                            今日短语

                                            1. become acquainted with 熟悉……

                                            2. cast off 摆脱

                                            3. peep out （花朵）开放

                                            4. by degrees 逐渐地

                                            5. follow up 跟进

                                            6. apply … to… 把……运用于……

                                            6. I improved, however, sensibly in this science, but not sufficiently to follow up any kind of conversation, /although I applied my whole mind to the endeavour, /for I easily perceived that, although I eagerly longed to discover myself to the cottagers, I ought not to make the attempt /until I had first become master of their language, /which knowledge might enable me to make them overlook the deformity of my figure, /for with this also the contrast perpetually presented to my eyes had made me acquainted.

                                            「sensibly」：能明显感觉到的，也就是说我的进步是非常明显的

                                            「in this science」：也就是在语言这方面

                                            「apply my whole mind to the endeavour」：把全部精力都用于（学习语言的）努力上

                                            「discover myself to the cottagers」：大家联系一下背景，怪物一直是藏身于小屋之中的，这家人都不知道怪物的存在，这里的“discover myself”可不是“发现自我”的意思，而是指怪物想在这家人面前现身，不再躲藏。

                                            「become master of」：精通……

                                            「deformity」：畸形

                                            这个句子有点长，大家跟着知米妞一起慢慢梳理一下。第一个“for”后面的句子，“for I easily perceived that…of my figure”是对前面“我”为什么要把全部精力用于学习语言的原因进行说明。最后的“for with this…”这里，“this”指代的是前面提到的“the deformity of my figure”，这里的提前也是为了使整个句子结构更加紧凑。还原一下就变成了“the contrast perpetually presented to my eyes had made me acquainted with this”。

                                            大意：不过，我还是在这方面取得了明显的进步，但是还不足以听懂他们之间的任何对话。可是我仍然把自己全部的精力都放在这个上面，因为我很明白，尽管我非常想和自己的邻居结交，但是在我掌握了他们的语言之前，我根本不能卤莽行事。只有我掌握了他们的知识以后，他们才可能会忽略我丑陋的外表。我和他们之间相貌上的巨大差异让我不得不这么想。


                                            7. From this time Felix was more employed, and the heart-movingindications of impending famine disappeared. 

                                            「heart-moving」：令人揪心的

                                            「indication」：迹象

                                            「impending famine」：即将到来的饥饿，也就是说这家人之前已经到了吃了上顿没下顿的状态了，随时可能会遭受饥饿。

                                            天气渐渐转暖了，菲利克斯也接到了更多的活，这家人的生活渐渐好转起来，怪物也对此很是欣慰。

                                            大意：费利克斯这时候更忙了，以前他们那种令人揪心的食不果腹的现象，已经不存在了。


                                            8. The old man, leaning on his son, walked each day at noon, when it did not rain, as I found it was called when the heavens poured forth its waters.

                                            「lean on」：靠在…..上

                                            「pour forth」：倾泻

                                            最后“as I found…”这里，“it”指代的就是前面提到的“rain”，“我”发现人们会用到这个词，当天上有水倒下来的时候。

                                            大意：只要天不下雨，老人每天都会扶着他的儿子在院子里散步。我知道‘下雨’是因为，每次天上有水倒下来时，他们就会说到这个词。
