彼得兔 Chapter 3

【0】 I have made many books about well-behaved people. Now, for a change, I am going to make a story about two disagreeable people, called Tommy Brock and Mr. Tod.
我写了很多讲好人的书，现在我要变一变，讲一个坏人的故事。故事里有两个坏人，一个叫汤米·布洛克，一个叫托德先生。

    【1】 Nobody could call Mr. Tod "nice." The rabbits could not bear him; they could smell him half a mile off. He was of a wandering habit and he had foxy whiskers; they never knew where he would be next. 「be of」：be of + 名词（比如这里的a wandering habit）表示句子主语在度量、大小、颜色、类别等方面的特征，而我们的托德的特征便是喜欢流浪。
    谁都不会把托德当好人。兔子们受不了他，半英里外都能闻到他的气味。他腮上长着狐狸般的胡须，性喜流浪，谁都不知道他下一刻会出现在哪里。

      【2】 One day he was living in a stick-house in the coppice（矮林）, causing terror to the family of old Mr. Benjamin Bouncer. Next day he moved into a pollard willow（秃头柳树） near the lake, frightening the wild ducks and the water rats.
      他今天来到灌木丛中，在一个树枝搭建的小房子里住下，把老本杰明·邦瑟先生一家子吓得够呛；明天又搬到湖边秃头柳树下，在野鸭和水老鼠当中引起恐慌。

        【3】 In winter and early spring he might generally be found in an earth amongst the rocks at the top of Bull Banks, under Oatmeal Crag.
        冬季和早春，他一般住在布尔河岸上，在燕麦岩下乱石间的地洞里。

        • earth是地球的意思，那in an earth在这里怎么理解呢？谢谢老师。
          在一个地洞里， earth可以用来表示“动物居所的洞穴”，可见朗文字典关于earth的第12个解释。

        【4】 He had half a dozen houses, but he was seldom at home. The houses were not always empty when Mr. Tod moved OUT; because sometimes Tommy Brock moved IN; (without asking leave).
        他有六所房子，但很少在家住。那些房子并不总是空着，托德先生搬出去时，汤米·布洛克会搬进来（未经允许）。

          【5】 Tommy Brock was a short bristly fat waddling person with a grin; he grinned all over his face. He was not nice in his habits. He ate wasp（黄蜂） nests and frogs and worms; and he waddled about by moonlight, digging things up.
          汤米·布洛克身材短小，身上长满刚毛，肥肥胖胖，摇摆着身子走路，咧着嘴，满面笑容。他习惯不好，爱吃黄蜂的蜂巢、青蛙和虫子，在月光下摇摆着身子到处挖东西。

            【6】 His clothes were very dirty; and as he slept in the day-time, he always went to bed in his boots. And the bed which he went to bed in, was generally Mr. Tod's. 「which he went to bed in」：定语从句部分，修饰前面的bed，需要注意的是从句中的bed为动词，表示上床睡觉。
            他的衣服很脏。由于他白天睡觉，所以上床时从不脱鞋，而他睡觉的床通常也是托德先生睡的床。

              【7】 Now Tommy Brock did occasionally eat rabbit-pie; but it was only very little young ones occasionally, when other food was really scarce. He was friendly with old Mr. Bouncer; they agreed in disliking the wicked otters（水獭） and Mr. Tod; they often talked over that painful subject.
              汤米·布洛克偶尔吃兔肉馅饼，但只在食物非常匮乏的时候才吃，而且只吃很嫩的小兔子。他跟老邦瑟先生很要好，都不喜欢邪恶的水獭们，也不喜欢托德先生。他们经常谈起这个痛苦的话题。

                【8】 Old Mr. Bouncer was stricken in years. He sat in the spring sunshine outside the burrow（洞穴）, in a muffler; smoking a pipe of rabbit tobacco.
                老邦瑟先生年纪大了，身体不好。他坐在洞穴外面，裹着头巾，在春日里晒着太阳，一边吸着烟袋，里面装着兔子烟。

                  【9】 He lived with his son Benjamin Bunny and his daughter-in-law Flopsy, who had a young family. Old Mr. Bouncer was in charge of the family that afternoon, because Benjamin and Flopsy had gone out.
                  他跟儿子本杰明和儿媳毛毛一起生活，他们已经有了自己的孩子。那天下午，老邦瑟先生负责看家，本杰明和毛毛外出有事去了。

                    【10】 The little rabbit-babies were just old enough to open their blue eyes and kick. They lay in a fluffy bed of rabbit wool and hay, in a shallow burrow（地洞）, separate from the main rabbit hole. To tell the truth--old Mr. Bouncer had forgotten them.
                    兔宝宝们才一点点大，刚会睁开蓝色的眼睛，踢踢小腿。他们躺在松软的床上，上面铺满了干草和兔毛。他们住在一个浅洞里，与大洞隔开。说实话，老邦瑟先生把他们忘在了脑后。

                      【11】 He sat in the sun, and conversed cordially with Tommy Brock, who was passing through the wood with a sack and a little spud which he used for digging, and some mole traps.
                      老邦瑟先生坐在阳光下，与汤米·布洛克亲切交谈。布洛克背着袋子，扛着锹，提着鼹鼠夹子，正好从林子路过。

                        【12】 He complained bitterly about the scarcity of pheasants' eggs（野鸡的蛋）, and accused Mr. Tod of poaching them. 「 complain（to sb）about sth」：to say that you are annoyed, unhappy or not satisfied about sb/sth，埋怨，发牢骚 再给大家举个栗子：I’m going to complain to the manager about this. 我要就这件事向经理投诉。 「accuse sb of doing」： to say that sb has done sth wrong or is guilty of sth，控诉，谴责 And the otters had cleared off all the frogs while he was asleep in winter- 「clear off」：本意有“消散；完成”，这里指的是水獭们把青蛙都捉完了。
                        他愤愤地抱怨说，野鸡蛋太少了，骂托德先生偷了蛋；而他在冬天睡觉时，水獭们把青蛙都捉光了。

                          【13】 "I have not had a good square meal for a fortnight, I am living on pig-nuts. I shall have to turn vegetarian and eat my own tail!" said Tommy Brock.
                          “我有两个星期没吃过一顿饱饭了，现在只靠吃点山胡桃果度日。这样下去，我得改吃素了，或者拿自己的尾巴当饭吃，”他说。

                          【14】 It was not much of a joke, but it tickled old Mr. Bouncer; because Tommy Brock was so fat and stumpy and grinning.
                          这个笑话并不怎么好笑，但老邦瑟先生却觉得有趣，因为汤米·布洛克一身肥肉，又矮又胖，咧着大嘴的样子实在滑稽。

                            【15】 So old Mr. Bouncer laughed; and pressed Tommy Brock to come inside, to taste a slice of seed-cake and "a glass of my daughter Flopsy's cowslip wine." Tommy Brock squeezed himself into the rabbit hole with alacrity.
                            所以老邦瑟先生大笑起来；他请汤米·布洛克进屋，尝一块种子饼，喝一杯“媳妇毛毛用流星花儿酿的酒”。于是汤米·布洛克挤进了兔子洞，动作很是敏捷。

                            【16】 Then old Mr. Bouncer smoked another pipe, and gave Tommy Brock a cabbage leaf cigar which was so very strong that it made Tommy Brock grin more than ever; and the smoke filled the burrow. Old Mr. Bouncer coughed and laughed; and Tommy Brock puffed and grinned.
                            随后，老邦瑟先生又抽了一袋烟，递了一支白菜叶雪茄给汤米·布洛克吸，那个烟劲道十足，让汤米·布洛克的嘴巴咧得更开了，而且满屋子灌满了烟雾。老邦瑟先生一边咳嗽，一边大笑；汤米·布洛克一边吐着烟圈，一边咧着嘴笑。

                              【17】 And Mr. Bouncer laughed and coughed, and shut his eyes because of the cabbage smoke..........
                              老邦瑟先生一边咳嗽，一边笑着，白菜烟熏得他闭上了眼睛。

                                【18】 When Flopsy and Benjamin came back--old Mr. Bouncer woke up. Tommy Brock and all the young rabbit-babies had disappeared!
                                当毛毛和本杰明回来的时候，老邦瑟先生醒了。汤米·布洛克和兔子宝宝们都没了踪影！

                                  【19】 Mr. Bouncer would not confess that he had admitted anybody into the rabbit hole. But the smell of badger was undeniable; and there were round heavy footmarks in the sand. He was in disgrace; Flopsy wrung his ears, and slapped him. 「 round heavy footmarks」：圆圆的重重的脚印。 所以你们看证据确凿：气味加脚印都证明有人来过，而且这位访客还“eat rabbit-pie”，尤其是 little young ones。 「wrung his ears」：wrung的原形是wring，意思是“拧，扭”， Flopsy拧了他的耳朵，还打了他一巴掌（slapped him）。 相信大家在看前面的时候，一定也在心里嘀咕了，Tommy Brock到底是什么来头呢？这里就直接揭露了他是一只獾（badger）

                                    兔子本杰明立刻出发去追汤米·布洛克。追踪他并不太困难：他慢吞吞地穿过树林，在弯曲的小路上留下了脚印；

                                      【21】 Here he had rooted up the moss and wood sorrel. There he had dug quite a deep hole for dog darnel（狗毒麦）; and had set a mole trap. A little stream crossed the way. Benjamin skipped lightly over dry-foot; the badger's heavy steps showed plainly in the mud.

                                        小路通往一个灌木丛，那儿的大树都被砍掉了。橡树桩上长满了绿叶，蓝色的风信子犹如一片海洋——可是让本杰明停下脚步的气味，并不是鲜花的气味。

                                          托德先生的木条房就在前面。这一次，托德竟然在家。证明他在家的不止是一股狐狸的气味，还有烟囱往外冒的烟，他家的烟囱是一只破水桶做的。

                                                                                            熟词生义

                                                                                            1.「stamp」：“邮票”的意思相信大家都非常熟悉啦，在文中作为动词，意思是“跺脚、用脚踩踏”：to put your foot down heavily and noisily on the ground.

                                                                                            原文： Benjamin stamped his foot, and bolted.

                                                                                            e.g. The audience were stamping and cheering. 观众又是跺脚又是欢呼。


                                                                                            2.「bag」：基本上咱们见到bag都是作为名词使用，在这里是不太常见的动词“把…装入袋中”：to put sth into bags.

                                                                                            原文： "No, no, no! He's bagged my family--Tommy Brock--in a sack--have you seen him?”

                                                                                            e.g. The fruit is washed, sorted and bagged at the farm. 水果在农场洗净分拣并装袋。


                                                                                            3.「compose」：这个单词常见的意思是“构成”、“作曲”，在文中则表示“使安定”：to manage to control your feelings or expression.

                                                                                            原文： "Which way?" "Cousin Benjamin, compose yourself."

                                                                                            e.g. I was so confused that I could hardly compose my thoughts. 我心烦意乱难以镇定情绪。

                                                                                            句子解析

                                                                                            1. Nobody could call Mr. Tod "nice." The rabbits could not bear him; they could smell him half a mile off. He was of a wandering habit and he had foxey whiskers; they never knew where he would be next.

                                                                                            「be of」：be of + 名词（比如这里的a wandering habit）表示句子主语在度量、大小、颜色、类别等方面的特征，而我们的托德的特征便是喜欢流浪。

                                                                                            大意是：谁都不会把托德当好人。兔子们受不了他，半英里外都能闻到他的气味，他腮上长着狐狸般的胡须，性喜流浪，谁都不知道他下一刻会出现在哪里。


                                                                                            2. His clothes were very dirty; and as he slept in the day-time, he always went to bed in his boots. And the bed ／which he went to bed in, was generally Mr. Tod’s.

                                                                                            「which he went to bed in」：定语从句部分，修饰前面的bed，需要注意的是从句中的bed为动词，表示上床睡觉。

                                                                                            大意是：他（ Tommy Brock ）的衣服很脏，由于他白天睡见，所以上床时从不脱鞋，而他睡觉的床通常也是托德先生睡觉的床。


                                                                                            3. He complained bitterly about the scarcity of pheasants' eggs（野鸡蛋）, and accused Mr. Tod of poaching them. And the otters had cleared off all the frogs while he was asleep in winter...

                                                                                            「 complain（to sb）about sth」：to say that you are annoyed, unhappy or not satisfied about sb/sth，埋怨，发牢骚

                                                                                            再给大家举个栗子：I’m going to complain to the manager about this. 我要就这件事向经理投诉。

                                                                                            「accuse sb of doing」： to say that sb has done sth wrong or is guilty of sth，控诉，谴责

                                                                                            「clear off」：本意有“消散；完成”，这里指的是水獭们把青蛙都捉完了。

                                                                                            大意是：他（ Brock）愤愤地抱怨说野鸡蛋太少了，骂托德先生偷了蛋；而他在冬天睡觉时水獭们把青蛙都捉光了......


                                                                                            4. Mr. Bouncer would not confess that he had admitted anybody into the rabbit hole. But the smell of badger was undeniable; and there were round heavy footmarks in the sand. He was in disgrace; Flopsy wrung his ears, and slapped him.

                                                                                            相信大家在看前面的时候，一定也在心里嘀咕了，Tommy Brock到底是什么来头呢？这里就直接揭露了他是一只獾（badger）。

                                                                                             「round heavy footmarks」：圆圆的重重的脚印。

                                                                                            獾的脚印大概是酱紫噢：

                                                                                            所以你们看证据确凿：气味加脚印都证明有人来过，而且这位访客还“eat rabbit-pie”，尤其是 little young ones。

                                                                                            「wrung his ears」：wrung的原形是wring，意思是“拧，扭”， Flopsy拧了他的耳朵，还打了他一巴掌（slapped him）。

                                                                                            大意是： Bouncer先生不肯承认他让人进了兔子洞，但獾的气味不容否认，而且沙地上还有圆脚印，他受了一顿责罚： Flopsy拧了他的耳朵，打了他一巴掌。


                                                                                            5. Benjamin Bunny set off at once after Tommy Brock. There was not much difficulty in tracking him; he had left his foot-mark and gone slowly up the winding footpath through the wood.

                                                                                            「set off」：to begin a journey，出发，动身，本杰明立刻出发去追“兔贩子”啦！

                                                                                            「have difficulty（in）doing」：the state or quality of being hard to do or to understand 做…很艰难，这里在前面加上否定词not，相当于have no difficulty in doing sth

                                                                                            「winding footpath」：这里的winding和风没有半毛钱关系噢，它是形容词，意思是“弯曲的，蜿蜒的”。

                                                                                            大意是：兔子本杰明立刻出发去追汤米.布鲁克。追踪他并不太困难，他慢吞吞地穿过树林，在弯曲的小路上留下了脚印。


                                                                                            6. Benjamin did so.”My Uncle Bouncer has displayed a lamentable want of discretion for his years;" said Peter reflectively, "but there are two hopeful circumstances. Your family is alive and kicking; and Tommy Brock has had refreshment. He will probably go to sleep, and keep them for breakfast.”

                                                                                            「a lamentable want of discretion」：lamentable 表示“令人失望的、令人惋惜的”；discretion表示“判断力、辨别力”，而a want of 则表示“缺乏”，这里字面意思是说令人遗憾地缺乏判断力，其实就是Benjamin在跟彼得表弟解释因为Bouncer年纪大了缺乏判断力才引“狼”入室的！

                                                                                            「Your family」：具体指的是七个兔宝宝，这里把它们当作是整体，所以谓语动词也用了单数is。

                                                                                            大意是：本杰明这样说：“ Bouncer老爷子年纪大了，做事太不谨慎。”彼得沉思着说，“看现在的情况，也许还有希望，一是孩子们暂时还平安，在袋子里乱踢呢；而是汤米.布鲁克已经吃过了点心，可能先睡觉，把他们留到明天当早餐吃。”

                                                                                            「sigh with relief」：轻松地叹了一口气
