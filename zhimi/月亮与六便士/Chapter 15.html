                                                                                  “听我说，”我对他说，“咱们一起回你的画室去吧。如果你自己把事办糟了，现在只好去负荆请罪。我认为你的妻子不是那种爱记仇的女人。”

                                                                                    【41】 "How can I go back to the studio?" he said wearily. "They're there. I've left it to them."
                                                                                    “我怎么能回画室呢？”他有气无力地说，“他们在那里呢。我把屋子让给他们了。”

                                                                                          The Moon and Sixpence  

                                                                                          Chapter 15 内容精讲

                                                                                          熟词生义

                                                                                          1. 「oath」：一般看到都知道是誓言，但是在这里是“咒骂”的意思：an offensive word or phrase used to express anger, surprise, etc.

                                                                                          原文：He received all inquiries about his feelings or his needs with a jibe, a sneer, or an oath.

                                                                                          e.g.  He ground out an oath. 他咬牙切齿地诅咒。


                                                                                          2. 「delicacy」：delicate的名词，大家是不是第一时间想到的释义是精致之类的，其实在这里是”佳肴，美味”的意思。

                                                                                          原文：But now he was wantonly extravagant in the purchase of delicacies, out of season and dear, which might tempt Strickland's capricious appetite.

                                                                                          e.g.  A pheasant is a delicacy we seldom can enjoy. 野鸡是我们很少能品尝到的美味。


                                                                                          今日短语

                                                                                          1. induce to 劝说，诱使

                                                                                          2. on the contrary 正相反

                                                                                          3. tempt one's appetite 吸引某人的胃口

                                                                                          4. remark on 评论，认可

                                                                                          5. burst out 突然说


                                                                                          句子解析

                                                                                          1. It needed a good deal of firmness and still more patience to induce him to come, but he was really too ill to offer any effective resistance to Stroeve's entreaties and to my determination.

                                                                                          「it needed sth to do sth」:做…需要…

                                                                                          「induce him to come」:劝诱Strickland到家里来（养病）

                                                                                          「too…to…」:太…而不能

                                                                                          「offer any effective resistance」：作出有效的抵抗，重病让Strickland一个大男人没有力气拒绝，按照他一贯对Stroeve的态度，他健康的话是万万不可能去的。

                                                                                          劝说Strickland搬到Stroeve家里来（induce him to come）需要很大的毅力和更多的耐心，幸而Strickland病得实在太重，对于Stroeve的央求和我的决心都做不出有效的抵抗了（offer any effective resistance）。

                                                                                          接下来的对比很有意思很切合人物形象，Stroeve是恳求，“我”是决心。这句话把三个人物之间复杂的朋友关系写了出来，不过去Stroeve家里养病这件事，之后引发了更多的转折和矛盾。


                                                                                          2. At one time it looked as though he could not live more than a few hours, and I am convinced that it was only through the Dutchman's doggedness that he pulled through. 

                                                                                          「at one time」：曾经有一个时期，一度，有一段时间Strickland 看起来好像几个小时都活不下去了

                                                                                          「I am convinced that…」：我相信...

                                                                                          「doggedness」：determined to continue with something even if it becomes difficult or dangerous，坚持不懈，顽强， Dutchman's doggedness指的也就是Stroeve的不懈照顾啦，真 救命恩人！

                                                                                          「pull through」：精神状况好转，恢复健康，正是Stroeve孜孜不倦地照顾，Strickland才能活下来啊

                                                                                          有一段日子，Strickland看起来连几个钟头也活不下去了，我毫不怀疑，他之所以能够活下来完全要归功于这位荷兰画家任劳任怨的照顾。


                                                                                          3. It was not that he was exacting and querulous; on the contrary, he never complained, he asked for nothing, he was perfectly silent; but he seemed to resent the care that was taken of him; he received all inquiries about his feelings or his needs with a jibe, a sneer, or an oath.

                                                                                          倒不是说他挑剔、抱怨（exacting and querulous），恰恰相反（on the contrary），他从来也不诉苦，从来不提出什么要求，他躺在那里一语不发。但是他似乎非常厌恨你对他的照顾（resent the care），谁要是问一句他觉得怎么样、有什么需要，他轻则挖苦你一句，重则破口大骂。

                                                                                          「exacting and querulous」：exact本身是精密的意思，形容词是指严格的，严苛的。

                                                                                          讲一下大家常用错的两个词组。“on the one hand”和“on the contrary”前者是不带有相反的意思的，只是指事物的不同方面，后者是指事物的相反方面。

                                                                                          「about his feelings or his needs」：是修饰“inquiries”的。


                                                                                          4. He was dexterous（灵巧的） to make him comfortable, and he exercised a cunning of which I should never have thought him capable to induce him to take the medicines prescribed by the doctor. 

                                                                                          「dexterous」：skilful with your hands; skilfully done，手巧的，熟练的，这里说的是Stroeve手脚麻利，把Strickland照顾得很好

                                                                                          「exercise」：动词“运用，使用”，Stroeve用了很巧妙的方法来照顾Strickland

                                                                                          「of which I should never have thought him capable」：用来修饰前面的a cunning（一种灵巧的方法），还原成简单的句子也就是 I should never have thought him capable of a cunning，我从没想过Stroeve还拥有这么巧妙的手段

                                                                                          「induce sb. to do」：诱使某人做…,这里是Stroeve用巧妙的办法使得Strickland吃药

                                                                                          「prescribed by the doctor」：医生给开的，用来修饰medicines

                                                                                          大意是：Stroeve的手脚非常利索，把病人弄舒舒服服，大夫开的药，他总是连哄带骗劝病人按时服用，我从来没想到他的手段这么巧妙。


                                                                                          5. I shall never forget the tactful patience with which he persuaded him to take nourishment. He was never put out by Strickland's rudeness; if it was merely sullen, he appeared not to notice it; if it was aggressive, he only chuckled（轻笑）.

                                                                                          「tactful」：还记得上文的tact嘛，这里指的是圆通的，得体的， tactful patience字面意思就是得体的耐心，这里说的是Stroeve对待Strickland极其耐心

                                                                                          「take nourishment」：服用营养品

                                                                                          「be put out」：被激怒，Stroeve从来没有因为Strickland的粗鲁恼怒过

                                                                                          「sullen」：愠怒的，不高兴的，如果Strickland有点儿不高兴，Stroeve就假装看不到。

                                                                                          大意是：我什么时候也忘不掉Stroeve劝说Strickland补充营养的那种耐心和手腕。不论Strickland对他多么没礼貌，他也从来不动火。如果对方只是郁闷懊恼，他就假装看不到；如果对方顶撞他，他只是一笑了之。


                                                                                          6. When I remarked on her competence, she told me with that pleasant little smile of hers that for a while she had worked in a hospital.

                                                                                          「remark on her competence」：本意是“就…发表意见”，在这里理解为称赞，我称赞她的能干

                                                                                          「with that pleasant little smile」：that是特指，指的是她脸上一贯常有的那种愉悦的微笑，这微笑也让人看不清她具体在想些什么

                                                                                          当我称赞她的能干时（remarked on her competence），她脸上露出惯有的微笑，告诉我她曾经在一家医院做过一段事 。


                                                                                          7. Strickland was a weird figure as he lay there, thinner than ever, with his ragged red beard and his eyes staring feverishly into vacancy; his illness seemed to have made them larger, and they had an unnatural brightness. 

                                                                                          「a weird figure」：古怪的样子

                                                                                          「raged red beard」：乱糟糟的红色胡子

                                                                                          「feverishly」：fever大家都知道，作为名词有“发热，狂热”之意，形容词形式是feverish-兴奋的，此处为副词形式，表示“兴奋地”

                                                                                          「vacancy」：半空，也有空虚的含义。形容词是vacant空虚的，空缺的。我们常说这个职位空缺了，“The position is vacant.”。

                                                                                          Strickland躺在床上，样子古怪吓人（was a weird figure），他的身躯比平常更加削瘦，红色的胡子乱成一团 ，眼睛兴奋地凝视着半空（feverishly into vacancy），因为生病，他的眼睛显得非常大，炯炯发光，但那光亮显得很不自然（had an unnatural brightness）。


                                                                                          8. I was convinced he had been drinking, and I smiled. I was on the point of chaffing him on his state.

                                                                                          「on the point of」:正要…的时候

                                                                                          「chaff」：to make jokes about sb in a friendly way（友善地开玩笑，打趣），相当于tease

                                                                                          「state」：指Stroeve这个人的状态

                                                                                          刚才我还不确定Stroe是不是喝醉了，现在进了屋看清了他的状态我确信他是喝醉了，那么到底是不是呢？

                                                                                          我一点儿也不怀疑，Stroeve一定是喝醉了。我对他笑了笑，正要打趣他这幅模样。


                                                                                          9. "You don't understand. She's in love with Strickland."

                                                                                          "What!" I was startled at this, but the idea had no sooner taken possession of me than I saw it was absurd.

                                                                                          这简直是个爆炸性新闻了！

                                                                                          「no sooner…than…」:这是比较常见的结构了，表示一…就…

                                                                                          「take possession of」：占据，这个消息一占据我,也就是我一听到这个消息就…

                                                                                          “你不了解，她爱上Strickland了。“

                                                                                          “什么！”我吓了一大跳；但是我一听到这个消息，就觉得这件事太荒谬了。


                                                                                          10. My first thought was that she had come to the end of her forbearance with his infatuation for Strickland, and, goaded by the latter's cynical behaviour, had insisted that he should be turned out.

                                                                                          「come to the end」：结束，终结，come to the end of her forbearance-忍耐终结，也就是忍受不下去了。

                                                                                          「his infatuation with Strickland」：这里的his指的是Stroeve’s, 而infatuation意思是迷恋-Stroeve对Strickland可不就是一腔热情嘛，但她老婆没法儿再忍受他的这种热心了。

                                                                                          「goad」：刺激，goaded by sth，即被…刺激，这里说的是我猜测她可能被Strickland的嘲讽行为刺激到了。

                                                                                          「turn out」：在这里的意思是撵走，逐出。再给大家举个例子：Surely nobody would suggest turning him out of the house. 当然不会有人建议把他从房子里赶出去。

                                                                                          我最初的想法是，她丈夫这种晕头晕脑地对Strickland倾心相待，叫她再也忍受不了了（come to the end of her forbearance），再加上Strickland总是冷嘲热讽（cynical behaviour）， 所以她坚决要把他赶走（be turned out）。
