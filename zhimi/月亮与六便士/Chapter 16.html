【0】 Still I could not take him seriously. I did not for a moment believe what he had told me. But he was in very real distress.
我仍然不能把他的话当真。我一点也不相信他告诉我的事，但是他的痛苦却是真真实实的。

    【1】 "Well, you've come here to talk to me about it. You'd better tell me the whole story."
    “好吧，既然你到这里来是要同我谈这件事，你就从头到尾给我说说吧。”

      【2】 "This afternoon I couldn't stand it any more. I went to Strickland and told him I thought he was quite well enough to go back to his own place. I wanted the studio myself."
      “今天下午我再也无法忍受了。我走到思特里克兰德跟前，对他讲，我觉得他身体已经完全恢复了，可以回自己的住处去了。我自己要用我的画室。”

        【3】 "No one but Strickland would have needed telling," I said. "What did he say?"
        “只有思特里克兰德才需要人家明明白白告诉他，”我说。“他怎么说的？”

          【4】 "He laughed a little; you know how he laughs, not as though he were amused, but as though you were a damned fool, and said he'd go at once. He began to put his things together. You remember I fetched from his room what I thought he needed, and he asked Blanche for a piece of paper and some string to make a parcel（包裹）."
          “他笑了笑。你知道他笑起来是什么样子，让人看起来不象是他觉得有什么事情好笑，而是叫你觉得自己是个大傻瓜。他说他马上就走，说着，就开始收拾东西。你还记得我从他的住处拿来一些我认为他用得着的东西。他叫勃朗什替他找一张纸，一条绳子，准备打一个包。”

            【5】 Stroeve stopped, gasping, and I thought he was going to faint. This was not at all the story I had expected him to tell me.
            施特略夫停住了，喘着气，我以为他要晕倒了。这根本不是我要他讲给我听的故事。

              【6】 "She was very pale, but she brought the paper and the string. He didn't say anything. He made the parcel and he whistled a tune（吹口哨）. He took no notice of either of us. His eyes had an ironic（讽刺的） smile in them. My heart was like lead.
              “她的脸色煞白，但还是把纸同绳子取来了。思特里克兰德一句话也不说，他一面包东西，一面吹着口哨，根本不理会我们两个人。他的眼角里寒着讥诮的笑意。我的心沉重得象一块铅块。

                【7】 I was afraid something was going to happen, and I wished I hadn't spoken. He looked round for his hat. Then she spoke: "`I'm going with Strickland, Dirk,' she said. `I can't live with you any more.'

                  【8】 "I tried to speak, but the words wouldn't come. Strickland didn't say anything. He went on whistling as though it had nothing to do with him." Stroeve stopped again and mopped his face（擦脸）. I kept quite still.
                  “我想说什么，可是一个字也说不出来。思特里克兰德也一句话不说。他继续吹着口哨，仿佛这一切同他都毫不相干似的。” 施特略夫又停了下来，开始揩汗。我默不作声。

                    【9】 I believed him now, and I was astounded. But all the same I could not understand. Then he told me, in a trembling voice, with the tears pouring down his cheeks, how he had gone up to her, trying to take her in his arms, but she had drawn away and begged him not to touch her.
                    我现在相信他了，我感到很吃惊。但是我仍然不能理解。 这时候他满面泪痕、声音抖抖索索地对我讲，他如何走到她跟前，想把她搂在怀里，她又如何把身体躲开，不叫他碰到自己。

                      【10】 He implored（恳求） her not to leave him. He told her how passionately he loved her, and reminded her of all the devotion he had lavished upon her. 「devotion」：great love, care and support for sb/sth，挚爱，深爱 Stroeve求妻子不要离开（implored her not to leave him），告诉她自己是多么爱她，叫她想一想自己对她的一片真情。 有关Stroeve的戏份，总是有各种恳求，乞求的单词。真是让人心酸又生气啊～ 「lavish sth on／upon sb/sth」:to give al lot of sth, often too much, 过分给予；滥施 ，这里可以还原成lavish all the devotion upon her，说的是Stroeve表白自己对妻子如何深情。举个栗子：She lavished most of her attention on her youngest son. 她对小儿子过分关心。 He spoke to her of the happiness of their life. He was not angry with her. He did not reproach her.

                                                                                                    今日短语

                                                                                                    1. make a parcel 打个包裹

                                                                                                    2. whistle a tune 吹口哨

                                                                                                    3. implore sb to do sth 恳求某人做某事

                                                                                                    4. tumble off 从......摔下来

                                                                                                    句子解析

                                                                                                    1. He implored her not to leave him. He told her how passionately he loved her, and reminded her of all the devotion he had lavished upon her. 

                                                                                                    「lavish sth on／upon sb/sth」:to give al lot of sth, often too much, 过分给予；滥施 ，这里可以还原成lavish all the devotion upon her，说的是Stroeve表白自己对妻子如何深情。举个栗子：She lavished most of her attention on her youngest son. 她对小儿子过分关心。

                                                                                                    「devotion」：great love, care and support for sb/sth，挚爱，深爱

                                                                                                    Stroeve求妻子不要离开（implored her not to leave him），告诉她自己是多么爱她，叫她想一想自己对她的一片真情。

                                                                                                    有关Stroeve的戏份，总是有各种恳求，乞求的单词。真是让人心酸又生气啊～


                                                                                                    2. Strickland's injurious calm robbed Stroeve of the rest of his self-control. Blind rage seized him, and without knowing what he was doing he flung himself on Strickland.

                                                                                                    联系上文，面对“夺妻”这件恶劣的事情，Strickland的态度依然随意到不行（"She can do as she chooses""She's not forced to come. "）真是让人不能忍啊！

                                                                                                    「injurious」：causing or likely to cause harm or damage，造成伤害的，

                                                                                                    「injurious calm」：造成伤害的镇定，即伤人的冷静
