【0】 I was not so much puzzled by Blanche Stroeve's action, for I saw in that merely the result of a physical appeal. I do not suppose she had ever really cared for her husband, and what I had taken for love was no more than the feminine response to caresses and comfort which in the minds of most women passes for it. 「take for」：信以为真 「no more than」：仅仅，只不过，原来我把女性对于爱抚（careness）和舒适的反应误认为是爱情，换句话说Stroeve妻子对于Stroeve并不是爱，是我误会了。 「pass for」：被误认为是，混淆 过去我认为她爱Stroeve，实际上只是男人的爱抚和生活的舒适在女人身上引起的自然反应，大多数女人都把这种反应当作爱情了。 「which」：指代前面的“response to caresses and comfort”，也就是说大部分的女人pass which (response to caresses and comfort）for it （love），她们把对于爱抚和舒适的反应误认为是爱情了。
勃朗什-施特略夫的行为还是容易解释的，我认为她做出那种事来只不过是屈服于肉体的诱惑。她对自己的丈夫从来就没有什么感情，过去我认为她爱施特略夫，实际上只是男人的爱抚和生活的安适在女人身上引起的自然反应。大多数女人都把这种反应当作爱情了。

    【1】 It is a passive feeling capable of being roused for any object, as the vine（藤曼） can grow on any tree; and the wisdom of the world recognises its strength when it urges a girl to marry the man who wants her with the assurance that love will follow.
    这是一种对任何一个人都可能产生的被动的感情，正像藤蔓可以攀附在随便哪株树上一样。因为这种感情可以叫一个女孩子嫁给任何一个需要她的男人，相信日久天长便会对这个人产生爱情，所以世俗的见解便断定了它的力量。

      【2】 It is an emotion made up of the satisfaction in security, pride of property, the pleasure of being desired, the gratification（喜悦） of a household, 「the satisfaction in security」：security本身是“安全，保护”的意思，结合语境，可以理解为衣食无忧的满足 「pride of property」：财产殷实的骄傲 「the pleasure of being desired」：被人需要的愉悦 「the gratification of a household」：家庭圆满的得意 and it is only by an amiable（亲切的） vanity that women ascribe to it spiritual value. 这几个短语其实只有两个东西，一个是面包，一个是被追求的愉悦。但是作者用了很具像的几个搭配把要说的内容充实了起来，我们在写作中也可以这样来使用。 关于世俗爱情，作者认为这只是女人们对物质保障的误解，所以在真正的爱情冲动面前，这些都不堪一击。 Chapter18全篇基本上都是作者在通过对这三个人的感情分析来传达他自身的感情观点。这一句是对Stroeve和他妻子的感情分析，说的丝丝入扣，尽管小说的时代过去了这么多年，但是这种看法依旧戳破了很多爱情的误区。 「it is…that…」:依然是大家熟悉的强调句式，去掉之后，我们稍微调整顺序，得到：Women ascribe to it spiritual value only by an amiable vanity. 所以女人赋予这种日久生情精神层面的价值，不过是出于一种无伤大雅的虚荣心而已，很不堪一击。
      但是说到底，这种感情是什么呢？它只不过是对有保障的生活的满足，对拥有家资的骄傲，对有人需要自己沾沾自喜，和对建立起自己的家庭洋洋得意而已；女人们禀性善良、喜爱虚荣，因此便认为这种感情极富于精神价值。

        【3】 It is an emotion which is defenceless against passion. I suspected that Blanche Stroeve's violent dislike of Strickland had in it from the beginning a vague element of sexual attraction. Who am I that I should seek to unravel the mysterious spirit of its own（纷繁难懂之处） of sex? 「seek to do」：尝试做…， seek to unravel 也就是尝试解开 「the mysterious intricacies of sex」：性的复杂神秘 这里的“who am I”不是问我是谁，而是我算老几，这样的说法。也可以从后面的成分来判断，这里的that后面还是I。
        但是在冲动的热情前面，这种感情是毫无防卫能力的。我怀疑勃朗什-施特略夫之所以非常不喜欢思特里克兰德，从一开始便寒有性的诱惑因素在内，可是性的问题是极其复杂的，我有什么资格妄图解开这个谜呢？

          【4】 Perhaps Stroeve's passion excited without satisfying that part of her nature, and she hated Strickland because she felt in him the power to give her what she needed. I think she was quite sincere when she struggled against her husband's desire to bring him into the studio; I think she was frightened of him, though she knew not why; and I remembered how she had foreseen disaster.
          或许施特略夫对她的热情只能刺激起，却未能满足她这一部分天性，她讨厌思特里克兰德是因为她感到他具有满足她这一需求的力量。当她拼命阻拦自己丈夫，不叫他把思特里克兰德带回家来的时候，我认为她还是真诚的；她被这个人吓坏了，尽管她自己也不知道为什么要怕他。我也记得她曾预言过思特里克兰德会带来灾难和不幸。

            【5】 I think in some curious way the horror which she felt for him was a transference of the horror which she felt for herself because he so strangely troubled her.
            我想，她对思特里克兰德的恐惧是她对自己的恐惧的一种奇怪的移植，因为他叫她迷惑不解，心烦意乱。

              【6】 His appearance was wild and uncouth; there was aloofness in his eyes and sensuality（好色） in his mouth; he was big and strong; he gave the impression of untamed passion; and perhaps she felt in him, too, 「give the impression of」：给人以…的印象 「untamed」：allowed to remain in a wild state，野性的，untamed passion that sinister element which had made me think of those wild beings of the world's early history when matter, retaining its early connection with the earth, seemed to possess yet a spirit of its own. 「sinister element」：凶兆性的成分 「retaining its early connection with the earth」：插入式短语，earth在此处指的是土地，不是地球，理解为同大地保持着原始的联系

                【7】 If he affected her at all, it was inevitable（必然的） that she should love or hate him. She hated him. And then I fancy that the daily intimacy with the sick man moved her strangely. She raised his head to give him food, and it was heavy against her hand; when she had fed him she wiped his sensual mouth and his red beard.
                如果思特里克兰德激发起她的感情来，不是爱就是恨，二者必居其一。当时她对思特里克兰德感到的是恨。 接着我又想像，她日夜同病人厮守，一定逐渐产生了一种奇怪的感情。她托着病人的头喂他食物，他的头沉甸甸地倚在她手上；在他吃过东西以后，她揩抹他的富于肉欲的嘴唇和火红的胡子。

                  【8】 She washed his limbs; they were covered with thick hair; and when she dried his hands, even in his weakness they were strong and sinewy（有力的）. His fingers were long; they were the capable, fashioning fingers of the artist; and I know not what troubling thoughts they excited in her.
                  她给他揩拭四肢，他的手臂和大退覆盖着一层浓密的汗毛。当她给他擦手的时候，尽管他病得非常虚弱，她也感觉得出它们如何结实有力。他的手指生得长长的，是艺术家那类能干的、善于塑造的手指。我无法知道它们在她心里引起什么样慌乱的思想。

                    【9】 He slept very quietly, without a movement, so that he might have been dead, and he was like some wild creature of the woods, resting after a long chase; and she wondered what fancies passed through his dreams.
                    他非常宁静地睡在那里，一动也不动，几乎和死人一样，他像是森林里的一头野兽，在一阵猛烈追猎后躺在那里休息；她在好奇地猜测，他正在经历什么奇异的梦境呢？

                      【10】 Did he dream of the nymph（女神） flying through the woods of Greece with the satyr（好色之徒） in hot pursuit? She fled, swift of foot and desperate, but he gained on her step by step, till she felt his hot breath on her neck; and still she fled silently, and silently he pursued, and when at last he seized her was it terror that thrilled her heart or was it ecstasy? 这一段的词汇难度不是很大，但是描写精彩，值得学习怎么样用简单的词汇来写出优美的文笔。 「swift of foot and desperate」：两个形容词并列，修饰状态。“gained on her step by step”这里作者没有用chase追赶她，而是用了gain获得，这样一个词，增加了很多紧张感，仿佛爪子已经可以够到她了。最后一句大家看出来问的是啥了么？不是when，而是“was it terror or ecstasy？”仔细看，其实只是被夹住了，开头是一个时间状语从句，然后or后面半句被置后了。

                      【11】 Blanche Stroeve was in the cruel grip of appetite. Perhaps she hated Strickland still, but she hungered for him, and everything that had made up her life till then became of no account. 「appetite」：这里不是“胃口”噢，而是a strong desire for sth，强烈的欲望， Blanche被强烈的欲望紧紧抓住了。 「hunger for」：极度需要，渴望得到，Blanche仍然讨厌Strickland，却渴望得到他。 「of no account」：相当于unimportant，组成她生活的所有事情都不重要了。 She ceased to be a woman, complex, kind and petulant（任性的）, considerate and thoughtless; she was a Maenad. She was desire.
                      如饥似渴的欲念毫不留情地把勃朗什-施特略夫抓在手里。也许她仍然恨着思特里克兰德，但是她却渴望得到他，在这以前构成她生活的那一切现在都变得一文不值了。她不再是一个女性了，不再是一个性格复杂的女性——既善良又乖戾，既谨慎又轻率；她成了迈那德，成了欲念的化身。

She may have had no particular feeling for him, but succumbed to his wish from propinquity（邻近） or idleness, to find then that she was powerless in a snare of her own contriving.
                        但是也许这都是我的臆测；可能她不过对自己的丈夫感到厌倦，只是出于好奇心（并无任何热情在内）才去我的思特里克兰德。可能她对他并没有特殊的感情，她之屈从于思特里克兰德的欲念只是由于两人日夜厮守、由于她厌烦无聊，而一旦同他接近以后，却发现陷入了自己编织的罗网里。

                          【13】 How did I know what were the thoughts and emotions behind that placid brow and those cool gray eyes?                                 【16】 The emotions common to most of us simply did not exist in him, and it was as absurd to blame him for not feeling them as for blaming the tiger because he is fierce and cruel.                                   【17】 I did not believe him capable of love. That is an emotion in which tenderness is an essential part, but Strickland had no tenderness either for himself or for others; there is in love a sense of weakness, a desire to protect, an eagerness to do good and to give pleasure – if not unselfishness, at all events a selfishness which marvellously（很好地） conceals itself; it has in it a certain diffidence.
                                  我不能相信思特里克兰德会爱上了勃朗什-施特略夫。我根本不相信这个人会爱上一个人。在爱这种感情中主要成分是温柔，但思特里克兰德却不论对自己或对别人都不懂得温柔。爱情中需要有一种软弱无力的感觉，要有体贴爱护的要求，有帮助别人、取悦别人的热情——如果不是无私，起码是巧妙地遮掩起来的自私；爱情包寒着某种程度的腼腆怯懦。

Love is absorbing; it takes the lover out of himself; the most clear- sighted, though he may know, cannot realise that his love will cease; it gives body to what he knows is illusion, and, knowing it is nothing else, he loves it better than reality.

He is no longer an individual, but a thing, an instrument to some purpose foreign to his ego.                                             The Moon and Sixpence  

                                            Chapter 18 内容精讲

                                            今日短语

                                            1. puzzled by one's action 被某人的行为所困惑

                                            2. make up of 由…构成

                                            3. rest after a long chase 经过长时间的追逐

                                            4. cease to 停止；不再出现某种情况

                                            5. devoid of 没有；缺乏的


                                            句子解析

                                            1. I do not suppose she had ever really cared for her husband, and what I had taken for love was no more than the feminine response to caresses and comfort which in the minds of most women passes for it. 

                                            「take for」：信以为真

                                            「no more than」：仅仅，只不过，原来我把女性对于爱抚（careness）和舒适的反应误认为是爱情，换句话说Stroeve妻子对于Stroeve并不是爱，是我误会了。

                                            「pass for」：被误认为是，混淆

                                            「which」：指代前面的“response to caresses and comfort”，也就是说大部分的女人pass which (response to caresses and comfort）for it （love），她们把对于爱抚和舒适的反应误认为是爱情了。

                                            过去我认为她爱Stroeve，实际上只是男人的爱抚和生活的舒适在女人身上引起的自然反应，大多数女人都把这种反应当作爱情了。


                                            2. It is an emotion made up of the satisfaction in security, pride of property, the pleasure of being desired, the gratification of a household, and it is only by an amiable vanity that women ascribe to it spiritual value.

                                            「the satisfaction in security」：security本身是“安全，保护”的意思，结合语境，可以理解为衣食无忧的满足

                                            「pride of property」：财产殷实的骄傲

                                            「the pleasure of being desired」：被人需要的愉悦

                                            「the gratification of a household」：家庭圆满的得意

                                            「it is…that…」:依然是大家熟悉的强调句式，去掉之后，我们稍微调整顺序，得到：Women ascribe to it spiritual value only by an amiable vanity. 所以女人赋予这种日久生情精神层面的价值，不过是出于一种无伤大雅的虚荣心而已，很不堪一击。

                                            这几句可以理解为：它只不过是对有保障的生活的满足（satisfaction in security），对拥有家资的骄傲（pride of property），对有人需要自己的沾沾自喜（pleasure of being desired），和对建立起自己的家庭的洋洋得意而已（gratification of a household），女人们禀性善良、喜爱虚荣， 因此便认为这种感情极富于精神价值。

                                            这几个短语其实只有两个东西，一个是面包，一个是被追求的愉悦。但是作者用了很具像的几个搭配把要说的内容充实了起来，我们在写作中也可以这样来使用。

                                            关于世俗爱情，作者认为这只是女人们对物质保障的误解，所以在真正的爱情冲动面前，这些都不堪一击。

                                            Chapter18全篇基本上都是作者在通过对这三个人的感情分析来传达他自身的感情观点。这一句是对Stroeve和他妻子的感情分析，说的丝丝入扣，尽管小说的时代过去了这么多年，但是这种看法依旧戳破了很多爱情的误区。


                                            3. Who am I that I should seek to unravel the mysterious intricacies of sex? Perhaps Stroeve's passion excited without satisfying that part of her nature, and she hated Strickland because she felt in him the power to give her what she needed.

                                            「seek to do」：尝试做…， seek to unravel 也就是尝试解开

                                            「the mysterious intricacies of sex」：性的复杂神秘

                                            可是性的问题是极其复杂的，我有什么资格妄图解开这个谜呢（unravel the mysterious intricacies of sex）？ 或许Stroeve对她的热情只能刺激起，却未能满足她这一部分天性，她讨厌Strickland是因为她感到他具有满足她这一需求的力量。

                                            这里的“who am I”不是问我是谁，而是我算老几，这样的说法。也可以从后面的成分来判断，这里的that后面还是I。


                                            4. He gave the impression of untamed passion; and perhaps she felt in him, too, that sinister element which had made me think of those wild beings of the world's early history when matter, retaining its early connection with the earth, seemed to possess yet a spirit of its own.

                                            「give the impression of」：给人以…的印象

                                            「untamed」：allowed to remain in a wild state，野性的，untamed passion

                                            「sinister element」：凶兆性的成分

                                            「retaining its early connection with the earth」：插入式短语，earth在此处指的是土地，不是地球，理解为同大地保持着原始的联系。

                                            这几句可以理解为：Strickland给人以热情狂放的印象（gave the impression of untamed passion）。也许她同我一样，在他身上感到某种邪恶的气质，这种气质使我想到宇宙初辟时的那些半人半兽的生物，那时宇宙万物同大地还保持着原始的联系（retaining its early connection），尽管是物质，却仿佛仍然具有精神的性质，


                                            5. She fled, swift of foot and desperate, but he gained on her step by step, till she felt his hot breath on her neck; and still she fled silently, and silently he pursued, and when at last he seized her was it terror that thrilled her heart or was it ecstasy?

                                            她拚命地逃跑，双腿如飞，但是他还是一步一步地离她越来越近（gained on her step by step），连他吹在她脖子上的热辣辣的呼吸她都感觉出来了。但是她仍然一声不出地向前飞跑，他也一声不出地紧紧追赶。最后，当她被他抓到手里的时候，使她浑身颤抖的是恐惧呢，还是狂喜呢？

                                            这一段的词汇难度不是很大，但是描写精彩，值得学习怎么样用简单的词汇来写出优美的文笔。

                                            「swift of foot and desperate」：两个形容词并列，修饰状态。“gained on her step by step”这里作者没有用chase追赶她，而是用了gain获得，这样一个词，增加了很多紧张感，仿佛爪子已经可以够到她了。最后一句大家看出来问的是啥了么？不是when，而是“was it terror or ecstasy？”仔细看，其实只是被夹住了，开头是一个时间状语从句，然后or后面半句被置后了。


                                            6. Blanche Stroeve was in the cruel grip of appetite. Perhaps she hated Strickland still, but she hungered for him, and everything that had made up her life till then became of no account. 

                                            「appetite」：这里不是“胃口”噢，而是a strong desire for sth，强烈的欲望， Blanche被强烈的欲望紧紧抓住了。

                                            「hunger for」：极度需要，渴望得到，Blanche仍然讨厌Strickland，却渴望得到他。

                                            「of no account」：相当于unimportant，组成她生活的所有事情都不重要了。

                                            如饥似渴的欲念毫不留情地把 Blanche Stroeve抓在手里。也许她仍然恨着Strickland，但是她却渴望得到他，在这以前构成她生活的那一切都变得一文不值了。


                                            7. But if one could be certain of nothing in dealing with creatures so incalculable as human beings, there were explanations of Blanche Stroeve's behaviour which were at all events plausible. 

                                            「incalculable」：very large or very great; too great to calculate，极大的，不可估量的，根据语境这里理解为不可捉摸的

                                            「at all events」：在任何情况下，无论如何

                                            「plausible」：reasonable and likely to be true，有道理的，可信的

                                            「which were at all events plausible」：从句部分修饰前面的 behaviour，即 Stroeve的行为是可以解释得通的

                                             然而尽管像探讨人这样无法捉摸的生物时，我们什么也不敢肯定，但对于Blanche Stroeve的一些行为还有一些解释是完全说得通的。


                                            8. Love is never quite devoid of sentimentality, and Strickland was the least inclined to that infirmity of any man I have known. I could not believe that he would ever suffer that possession of himself which love is; he could never endure a foreign yoke.

                                            「be devoid of」：缺少，缺乏

                                            「be inclined to」：倾向于…，前面有the least 来修饰的话，表示否定的含义，说Strickland是最不容易患这种病症（多愁善感）的人。

                                            「suffer that possession」：这个搭配是不是难懂了。其实possession指的是感情所拥有的让人疯狂的东西。

                                            「never endure a foreign yoke」：foreign在这里不是外国，是外界，或者说不相干的，yoke是束缚，羁绊。所以可以理解为无法忍受外界加给他的任何桎梏。

                                            这几句的意思是：爱情从来免不了多愁善感（devoid of sentimentality），而Strickland却是我认识的人中最不易犯这种病症的人（inclined to that infirmity）。我不相信他在任何时候会害那种爱情的通病——如醉如痴、神魂颠倒，他从来不能忍受外界加给他的任何桎梏（endure a foreign yoke）。
