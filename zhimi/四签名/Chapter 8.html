【0】 "My father was, as you may have guessed, Major John Sholto, once of the Indian Army. He retired some eleven years ago and came to live at Pondicherry Lodge in Upper Norwood.
“我的父亲，你们会猜想到，就是过去在印度驻军里的约翰·舒尔托少校。他大约是在十一年前退休后，才到上诺伍德的樱沼别墅来的。

    【1】 He had prospered in India and brought back with him a considerable sum of money, a large collection of valuable curiosities, and a staff of native servants. With these advantages he bought himself a house, and lived in great luxury. My twin-brother Bartholomew and I were the only children.
    他在印度很发了些财，带来一大笔钱和一批贵重的古玩，还有几个印度仆人。有了这些好条件，他就买了一所房子，过着非常优裕的生活。我和巴索洛谬是孪生兄弟，我父亲只有我们这两个孩子。

      【2】 "I very well remember the sensation which was caused by the disappearance of Captain Morstan. We read the details in the papers, and knowing that he had been a friend of our father's we discussed the case freely in his presence.
      　　“我还很清楚地记得摩斯坦上尉的失踪在社会上所引起的轰动，详情还是我们从报纸上读到的呢。因为我们知道他是父亲的朋友，所以常常无拘无束地在他面前讨论这件事。

        【3】 He used to join in our speculations as to what could have happened. Never for an instant did we suspect that he had the whole secret hidden in his own breast, that of all men he alone knew the fate of Arthur Morstan. 「Never for an instant」:一刻也没有。这里否定词never放在句首，要用倒装句型,用来表示强调。看来Major John Sholto的儿子们完全蒙在鼓里啊~ 啧啧，Major John Sholto隐藏秘密的本事真是厉害，姜还是老的辣啊 这个句子的结构比较简单，后面跟了两个「that」从句，表示这两件事都是我们从未起过疑心的事。 第一个「that」:这整个的秘密却藏在他一个人的心里。 第二个「that」:只有他一个人知道阿瑟·摩斯坦的结局。
        他有时也和我们揣测这件事是怎么发生的，我们丝毫也没有疑心到这整个的秘密却藏在他一个人的心里——只有他一个人知道阿瑟·摩斯坦的结局。

          【4】 "We did know, however, that some mystery, some positive danger, overhung our father. He was very fearful of going out alone, and he always employed two prize-fighters（拳击手）to act as porters at Pondicherry Lodge. Williams, who drove you tonight, was one of them. He was once lightweight champion of England.
          　　“可是我们确也知道有些秘密——有些恐怖的事——存在我父亲心里。他平常不敢一人独自出门，他还雇了两个拳击手为樱沼别墅看门。今天为你们赶车的威廉就是其中的一个，他过去是英国轻量级拳赛的冠军。

            【5】 Our father would never tell us what it was he feared, but he had a most marked aversion （反感）to men with wooden legs. 「what it was he feared」:他所惧怕的东西 「most」:这里不是形容词最高级哦，它和very的意思是一样的 「marked aversion」:显著的反感，这里就是指Major John Sholto凡是看到木腿人就心慌慌了。 我父亲从来不告诉我们他所怕的是什么，他对装有木腿的人尤其加以戒备。 这事有些蹊跷呀，有没有觉得Major John Sholto身上有些可疑之处？
            我父亲从来不告诉我们他所怕的是什么，他对装有木腿的人尤其加意地戒备。

              【6】 On one occasion he actually fired his revolver at a wooden legged man, who proved to be a harmless tradesman canvassing for orders. 「on one occasion」:有一次 「fire at sb」:对某人开枪，这里就是指Major John Sholto对一个木腿人开枪。 「prove to be」:事实证明，这里就是“真相其实是”的意思。 原来那个木腿人就是一个无害的（harmless）,也就是对Major John Sholto根本不构成威胁的、兜揽生意的商贩（trademan for canvassing for orders）。 We had to pay a large sum to hush the matter up. 「pay a large sum 」:花一大笔钱，这里的 “sum”是“金额”的意思，也就是pay a large sum of money. 「hush sth up」:掩盖真相，这里就是大家花钱把Major John Sholto误伤别人这件事封口啦。 My brother and I used to think this a mere whim of my father's, but events have since led us to change our opinion.

                【7】 "Early in 1882 my father received a letter from India which was a great shock to him. He nearly fainted at the breakfast-table when he opened it, and from that day he sickened to his death.
                　　“一八八二年春间，我父亲接到了一封从印度来的信，这封信对他是一个很大的打击。他在早餐桌上读完这封信后几乎晕倒，从那天起他就病倒了，一直到他死去。

                  【8】 What was in the letter we could never discover, but I could see as he held it that it was short and written in a scrawling hand. He had suffered for years from an enlarged spleen（脾）, but he now became rapidly worse, and towards the end of April we were informed that he was beyond all hope, and that he wished to make a last communication to us.

                    【9】 "When we entered his room he was propped up with pillows and breathing heavily.                                                             熟词生义

                                                            1. 「curiosity」:这个词除了表示“好奇心”，它还有一个意思：珍品即 something that is interesting because it is rare and unusual。所以老古董就可以用这个词啦~

                                                            原文： He had prospered in India and brought back with him a considerable sum of money, a largecollection of valuable curiosities, and a staff of native servants.

                                                            e.g.Cars like mine are curiosities nowadays. 像我拥有的这种车如今实属罕见了。


                                                            2.「tip」:这个词除了大家常见的“小贴士”“小费”的意思外，还可以表示“覆盖…的尖端”，即to cover the end of something.

                                                            原文： See that chaplet tipped with pearls beside the quinine-bottle.

                                                            e.g.The giraffe was killed with a spear that had been tipped with poison.

                                                            长颈鹿是被尖上涂有毒药的长矛杀死的。


                                                            3. 「seal」:这个词除了表示“海豹”，它还可以作动词表示“密封”。

                                                            原文：But my lips are sealed.

                                                            e.g.Seal the package with tape. 用透明胶带把包裹封起来。


                                                            4.「rifle」:这个词除了作名词，表示“来福枪”，它还可以作动词，表示“匆忙翻找”，即to search quickly through something, often in order to steal something.

                                                            原文：His cupboards and boxes had been rifled.

                                                            e.g.The safe had been rifled and the diamonds were gone.

                                                            保险柜被洗劫了，钻石不翼而飞。


                                                            今日短语

                                                            1. hush sth up 掩埋真相

                                                            2. sickened to death 病死

                                                            3. be informed that...得知

                                                            4. beyond all hope毫无希望;不可挽救

                                                            5. prop up支撑

                                                            6. ponder over sth 沉思

                                                            7. conjure up sth 想象出

                                                            8. associate with 联想


                                                            句子解析

                                                            1. Never for an instant did we suspect that he had the whole secret hidden in his own breast, that of all men he alone knew the fate of Arthur Morstan.

                                                            「Never for an instant」:一刻也没有。这里否定词never放在句首，要用倒装句型,用来表示强调。看来Major John Sholto的儿子们完全蒙在鼓里啊~

                                                            这个句子的结构比较简单，后面跟了两个「that」从句，表示这两件事都是我们从未起过疑心的事。

                                                            第一个「that」:这整个的秘密却藏在他一个人的心里。

                                                            第二个「that」:只有他一个人知道阿瑟·摩斯坦的结局。

                                                            啧啧，Major John Sholto隐藏秘密的本事真是厉害，姜还是老的辣啊


                                                            2. Our father would never tell us what it was he feared, but he had a most marked aversion to men with wooden legs.

                                                            「what it was he feared」:他所惧怕的东西

                                                            「most」:这里不是形容词最高级哦，它和very的意思是一样的

                                                            「marked aversion」:显著的反感，这里就是指Major John Sholto凡是看到木腿人就心慌慌了。

                                                            我父亲从来不告诉我们他所怕的是什么，他对装有木腿的人尤其加以戒备。

                                                            这事有些蹊跷呀，有没有觉得Major John Sholto身上有些可疑之处？


                                                            3. On one occasion he actually fired his revolver at a wooden legged man, //who proved to be a harmless tradesman canvassing for orders. We had to pay a large sum to hush the matter up.

                                                            「on one occasion」:有一次

                                                            「fire at sb」:对某人开枪，这里就是指Major John Sholto对一个木腿人开枪。

                                                            「prove to be」:事实证明，这里就是“真相其实是”的意思。

                                                            原来那个木腿人就是一个无害的（harmless）,也就是对Major John Sholto根本不构成威胁的、兜揽生意的商贩（trademan for canvassing for orders）。

                                                            「pay a large sum 」:花一大笔钱，这里的 “sum”是“金额”的意思，也就是pay a large sum of money.

                                                            「hush sth up」:掩盖真相，这里就是大家花钱把Major John Sholto误伤别人这件事封口啦。


                                                            4. When we entered his room he was propped up with pillows and breathing heavily. He besought us to lock the door and to come upon either side of the bed. Then grasping our hands he made a remarkable statement to us in a voice //which was broken as much by emotion as by pain.

                                                            「be propped up」:撑起，这里就是指他的头用枕头撑起来，换句话说，就是：他倚靠在高高的枕头上。

                                                            「either side of the bed」:床的两边

                                                            「made a remarkable statement」:本意是发表一个非凡的讲话，在这里就可以理解为Major John Sholto讲了一件惊人的事。

                                                            「which」:修饰前面的voice,这个声音是破碎的（broken），即断断续续的.

                                                            「as...as...」:同级比较，“和……一样”，在这里可以理解为既是因为情绪激动，又是因为病痛。

                                                            连在一起：当我们走进房间的时候，他呼吸急促地倚在高枕上面。他叫我们把门锁上，到床的两旁来。他紧握我们的手，因为痛苦难堪而又感情激动，所以断断续续地告诉了我们一件惊人的事。


                                                            5. The cursed greed //which has been my besetting sin through life //has withheld from her the treasure, half at least //of which should have been hers. And yet I have made no use of it myself, so blind and foolish a thing is avarice(贪婪).

                                                            第一句话先拎一下主干：The greed has withheld from her treasure.

                                                            「the cursed greed」:讨厌的贪婪，cursed在这里并不是“被诅咒”的意思，而是表示“讨厌的”，抒发一下说话者内心的愤怒。

                                                            「which」:第一个定语从句，修饰前面的greed,指贪婪是困扰我一生的罪恶。

                                                            「which」:第二个定语从句，修饰前面的treasure,指宝物的一半本应该归Miss Morstan.

                                                            「so」:放在句首要倒装哦。这句话等于Such a blind and foolish thing is avarice~

                                                            连在一起：由于我一生不可宽恕的贪心，使她没能得到这些宝物——其中至少一半是属于她的。可是我也未曾利用过这些宝物——贪婪真是极愚蠢的行为。


                                                            6. Morstan had sprung out of his chair in a paroxysm of anger, when he suddenly pressed his hand to his side, his face turned a dusky hue, and he fell backward, cutting his head against the corner of the treasure chest. When I stooped over him I found, to my horror, that he was dead.

                                                            「spring out of his chair」:从他椅子上跳起来

                                                            「in a paroxysm of anger」:突然怒火冲天

                                                            「when」:在这里表示“这时”，因为根据下文，剧情突然逆转，Morstan心脏病突发了。

                                                            「his face turned a dusky hue」:面色阴暗

                                                            「stoop over」:弯腰，这里就是指Major John Sholto看到Morstan心脏病发了，他弯腰把他扶起来。

                                                            连在一起就是：摩斯坦在盛怒之下从椅子上跳了起来，随后忽然把手放在胸侧，面色阴暗，向后跌倒，头撞在宝箱的角上。当我弯腰扶他的时候，使我感到万分惊恐，他竟已死了。


                                                            7. We both stared round at the window behind us upon which his gaze was fixed. A face was looking in at us out of the darkness. We could see the whitening of the nose where it was pressed against the glass. It was a bearded, hairy face, with wild cruel eyes and an expression of concentrated malevolence（恶毒）.

                                                            「his gaze was fixed」:眼神凝滞，这里就突出了Major John Sholto看到窗外的人，惊恐万分。

                                                            后面的两句话画面感极强，黑暗里有一个面孔正向我们凝视。是不是后背发凉了~

                                                            我们可以看见他那在玻璃上被压得变白的鼻子（the whitening of the nose）。长着胡子（bearded）,多毛（hairy ）的脸，两只凶狠的眼睛(wild cruel eyes)，还有凶恶的表情(an expression of concentrated malevolence)。
