                                                          它们一般生长在热带海洋潮涨潮落的潮间地带。


                                                          2.ague：疟疾

                                                          疟疾是经按蚊叮咬或输入带疟原虫者的血液而感染疟原虫所引起的虫媒传染病。本病主要表现为周期性规律发作，全身发冷、发热、多汗，长期多次发作后，可引起贫血和脾肿大。


                                                          3.the Queen's shilling 女王先令

                                                          The King's shilling, sometimes called the Queen's shilling when the Sovereign is female.国王的先令，当君主为女性时，有时被称为女王先令。是一个历史性的俚语，指的是在18和19世纪为英国武装部队招募一名先令的恳切支付，尽管 这种做法可以追溯到英国内战结束。"take the King's shilling" ---“得到国王的先令”就是同意担任皇家海军或英国陆军的水手或士兵。这种做法在1879年正式停止，尽管该术语仍然是非正式使用的。


                                                          4.goose-step：鹅步操

                                                          　  据说，阅兵正步源于旧普鲁士陆军的操演步伐，士兵们为了达到“趾高气扬”的效果，模仿公鹅的步法。所以，这种步法也被称为“鹅步”

                                                          “鹅步”一词最初指的是英国军事演习，其中一条腿一次来回摆动而不弯曲膝盖。显然，只用一条腿站立提醒士兵们鹅经常站立。这个词后来在第一次世界大战期间被应用于德语速记中，现在与纳粹德国联系紧密。因此，这个词在一些讲英语的国家中具有贬义的含义。


                                                          熟词生义

                                                          1.「sound」：这个我们一般会把它翻译成“声音”或者是和“good”一起组成“sounds good”表示听起来不错。但是在文中它的意思是“健全的”“完好的”，即“good”。

                                                          原文：My companion lounged in his armchair with his usual listless expression, while Small sat stolidly opposite to him with his wooden leg cocked over his sound one.

                                                          e.g.Although the car is basically sound, I was worried about certain areas.

                                                          虽然这辆汽车基本状况完好，但我担心某几个部分。


                                                          2.「post」：一般情况下，我们把这个词翻译成“邮政”，但在这句话里它的意思是“岗位，工作”的意思，即“work”。

                                                          原文：. To make a long story short, the colonel recommended me strongly for the post.

                                                          e.g.Soon they assigned him to a new post.

                                                          不久，他们派他到一个新的工作岗位。


                                                          3.「cell」：在文中他是“牢房”，而不是“细胞”的意思呦~

                                                          原文：I would rather swing a score of times, or have one of Tonga's darts in my hide, than live in a convict's cell and feel that another man is at his ease in a palace with the money that should be mine.

                                                          e.g.She was taken to a small cell where she was subjected to a brutal beating.

                                                          她被带到一个小牢房里被狠狠打了一顿。


                                                          4.「quarter」：这个词在文中的意思是“住处，宿舍，营房 ”，并不是我们熟知的“四分之一”哦~

                                                          原文：The pay was fair, I had comfortable quarters, and altogether I was content to spend the remainder of my life in indigo-planting.

                                                           e.g.Mckinnon went down from deck to the officers' quarters.

                                                          麦金农走下甲板，来到军官们的住处。


                                                          5. 「bank」：大家还记得这个词吗？它在文中的意思是“岸”哦~可能我们更熟悉的意思是“银行”。

                                                          原文：.What with the shock and the loss of blood, I fainted, and should have been drowned if Holder had not caught hold of me and paddled for the bank.

                                                          e.g.We moored the boat at the path, and jumped onto the bank.

                                                          在小径旁边，船停住了，我们都跳上了岸。


                                                          今日短语

                                                          1.cloud over：脸色变得阴沉

                                                          2.a heap of ：很多，大量

                                                          3.be destined to do:注定要做

                                                          4.down on my luck :倒霉

                                                          5.a blessing in disguise：因祸得福

                                                          6.happened to be：碰巧是

                                                          7.drop into：顺便去某地;把…丢进


                                                          句子解析

                                                          1."There goes the reward!" said he gloomily. "Where there is no money there is no pay.”

                                                          「There goes the reward」：倒装句。奖金也没了。

                                                          There be（的各种形式）+主语（+地点或时间状语）

                                                          e.g.There was a drop in the temperature.温度下降了

                                                          过去分词或现在分词+be的各种形式+主语+……

                                                          e.g.Scattered on the floor were several books and magazines.几本书和杂志散落在地板上。

                                                          here、there、now、then、thus等副词开头的句子可构成完全倒装。条件是谓语动词是不及物动词，如arise、be、come、exist、go、follow等。方位词在句首，主语是名词，全部倒装：

                                                          e.g.Here comes our headmaster.我们的校长来了。

                                                          Here is your key.这是你的钥匙。

                                                          需要注意的是，当主语是代词时，不能构成倒装：

                                                          e.g.Here it is. 在这里。

                                                          「Where there is no money there is no pay」：和「where there is a will, there is a way」：结构相同，可以说是个条件状语从句。where 是关系副词，引导一个状语从句修饰主句。

                                                          大意：  他郁闷地说道：“这一来，奖金也完了！箱子里没有宝物也就等于没有奖金了。”


                                                          2."Mr. Thaddeus Sholto is a rich man," I said; "he will see that you are rewarded, treasure or no."

                                                          「he will see that you are rewarded, treasure or no.」：整句话可以拓展为：he will see that you should be rewarded, no matter whether treasure is there or not.

                                                          「see」：了解，理解

                                                          大意：我道：“塞笛厄斯·舒尔托先生是个有钱的人，不管宝物有没有，他都会给你们酬劳的。”


                                                          3.My companion lounged in his armchair with his usual listless expression, while Small sat stolidly opposite to him with his wooden leg cocked over his sound one. As I exhibited the empty box he leaned back in his chair and laughed aloud.

                                                          「cock over」：翘起

                                                          「sound」：完好的

                                                          「stolidly」：神经麻木的，冷淡的

                                                          「lean back」：靠着

                                                          福尔摩斯在没案子可查时仿佛永远都是百无聊赖的样子“ lounged in ”闲坐着，“with his usual listless expression”带着一副无精打采的表情。而斯茂则是破罐子破摔“反正童格也死了，死无对证的事，我还怕什么？况且这些警察也找不到宝藏！”在华生告诉他们宝箱是空的之后更是发出了得逞般的大笑。

                                                          大意：福尔摩斯仍象往常一样，懒洋洋地坐在他的椅子上，面对着呆呆地坐在那儿的斯茂。斯茂把那条木腿搭在好腿上面。当我把空箱子给大家看的时候，他倚着椅子放声大笑起来。


                                                           4. "Yes, I have put it away where you shall never lay hand upon it," he cried exultantly. "It is my treasure, and if I can't have the loot I'll take damed good care that no one else does."

                                                          「lay hand on」：找到；得到。

                                                          「take care」：小心，坚持到底。

                                                          「if I can't have the loot I'll take damed good care...... 」：这是一个由引导词if引导的状语从句叫做条件状语从句。在英文中，条件是指某一件事情实现之后（状语从句中的动作，在句中是“can't have the loot”），其它事情（主句中的动作，在句中是“ take damed good care”）才能发生，通常译作“假如”。（注意：在含有条件状语从句的复合句中，主句要用一般将来时态、祈使句或情态动词，从句要用现在时态）

                                                          e.g. If you ask him,he will help you. 如果你请他帮忙，他会帮你的。

                                                          到这里斯茂已经为宝物争红了眼，尽管他的后半生很可能在监狱里度过，他也仍旧坚持除了自己以外，旁人没有资格得到宝物。

                                                          大意：  斯茂狂笑着喊道：“不错，我已经把宝物放到你们永远摸不到的地方去了。宝物是属于我的，如果我得不到手，我就得想办法叫谁也摸不着。”


                                                           5.I have acted all through for them as much as for myself. It's been the sign of four with us always. Well, I know that they would have had me do just what I have done, and throw the treasure into the Thames rather than let it go to kith or kin of Sholto or Morstan.

                                                          「act for」：代表（某人），代替（某人）执行某事

                                                          「kith or kin」：亲朋好友

                                                          「I know that they would have had me do just what I have done」：would have done虚拟语气，表示对过去事情的假设，意思是“本来会做”。

                                                          e.g.I would have told you all about the boy’s story,but you didn’t ask me.我本来会告诉你这个小男孩的故事，但是你没有问我。

                                                          「had me do」：即让我做。

                                                          e.g.Maximus: What would you have me do, Caesar? 麦克西姆：您想让我做什么？

                                                          大意：我代表他们三人和我自己把宝物处理了。这样正符合我们四个人签名时所发的誓言。我知道他们三人必然同意我这样办——宁可把宝物沉到泰晤士河河底，也不叫宝物落到舒尔托或摩斯坦的子女或亲属的手里。


                                                          6."The man that was clever enough to hunt me down is clever enough to pick an iron box from the bottom of a river. Now that they are scattered over five miles or so, it may be a harder job. It went to my heart to do it though. I was half mad when you came up with us. However, there's no good grieving over it. I've had ups in my life, and I've had downs, but I've learned not to cry over spilled milk."

                                                          「The man......」：指的是福尔摩斯。那个聪明到可以抓住我的人也可以轻易地从河底找到铁箱。

                                                          「hunt down」：搜索直至找到（某物）

                                                          「scattered over」：把…洒（撒）在…上

                                                          「come up with」：追赶上 

                                                          「grieve over」：悼念;对…感到伤心

                                                          「I've had ups in my life, and I've had downs」：I've had ups and downs in my life, 意为：我这一生过得跌宕起伏。下文就会详细阐述斯茂的前半生到底有多辛酸了。

                                                          「cry over spilled milk」：为打翻的牛奶流泪（为过去的失败而懊丧），用一个成语来概括就是“覆水难收”，这个成语希望大家记一下，相信会为你的作文增色不少。

                                                          看到斯茂说出“覆水难收，我绝不后悔”这里知米妞不禁疑惑：对斯茂来说，是生存比较重要还是宝藏比较重要呢?

                                                          大意：“你们有本领把我追寻着，你们就有本领去捞一只铁箱子。现在我已把宝物散投在长达五英里的一段河道里，捞起来就不太容易了。我也是横了心干的，当我看到你们追上来的时候，我几乎都要发疯了。惋惜是没有什么用处的，我这一辈子的命运有盛有衰，我可向来没有事后追悔过。”


                                                          7."Twenty long years in that fever-ridden swamp（热病猖狂的湿地）, all day at work under the mangrove-tree, all night chained up in the filthy convict-huts（牢房）, bitten by mosquitoes, racked with ague, bullied by every cursed black-faced policeman who loved to take it out of a white man. That was how I earned the Agra treasure, and you talk to me of justice/ because I cannot bear to feel that I have paid this price /only that another may enjoy it! I would rather swing a score of times, or have one of Tonga's darts in my hide, than live in a convict's cell and feel that another man is at his ease in a palace with the money that should be mine."

                                                          「all day ...」「all night...」： 这两个短语就说明了斯茂整日整夜地干苦活累活，当囚犯的日子没有自由也没有尊严......

                                                          「bitten by...」「bullied by...」：两个结构相同的动词短语说明了斯茂的苦役生活条件恶劣，在那个医疗条件十分匮乏的年代，疟疾是一种死亡率很高的疾病吧，加之禁卒严刑拷打，生存变成了难题。

                                                          「pay the price」：付出代价;得到报应。

                                                          「only that」：却是

                                                          「would rather do sth. than do sth.」：宁愿做…而不愿做…

                                                          e.g.I would rather die than do that. 我死也不会那么做。

                                                          「a score of times」：几十次

                                                          「hide」：人的皮肤；这个词译作“皮肤”不大常见的，大家了解一下就可以啦~

                                                          「cell」：牢房

                                                          「at one's ease」：安逸，舒适；无拘无束

                                                          试想想，谁能做到在干二十年的苦工历经百般磨难之后，终于得到宝箱仍旧能够心如止水？那可是足够他们后半生锦衣玉食的财宝啊！并且谁还能在被别人骗走宝箱之后继续忍受苦役生活？当然是要复仇啦！

                                                          这里是影片中斯茂做苦役的片段，以及鞭打他们的禁卒。


                                                          大意："整整二十年，在那热病猖狂的湿地里住着，白天整日在红树下面做苦工，夜晚被锁在污秽的囚棚里，镣铐加身，被蚊子咬着，被疟疾折磨着，受着喜欢拿白种人泄愤的每个可恶的黑脸禁卒的种种凌辱，这是我赚到阿格拉宝物的代价，而你却要来同我讲什么公道？就因为我忍不了我付出了这么多代价却是为了让另外一个人享受成果！我宁愿被绞死或吃童格一毒刺，也不甘心在牢狱里活着而叫另外一个人拿着应当是我的钱去快乐逍遥！"


                                                           8.Small had dropped his mask of stoicism, and all this came out in a wild whirl of words, while his eyes blazed, and the handcuffs clanked together with the impassioned movement of his hands.

                                                          「dropped his mask of stoicism」：意为丢下了他淡然的面具。也就是说斯茂变得不再淡然沉默。

                                                          「a whirl of words」：一连串的话语。

                                                          大意：这时斯茂已经不象以前沉默了，他滔滔不绝地倾泻出这些话来。他两眼发亮，手铐随着激动的双手震得作响。


                                                           9."Well, sir, you have been very fair-spoken to me, though I can see that I have you to thank that I have these bracelets upon my wrists. Still, I bear no grudge for that. It is all fair and above-board. If you want to hear my story, I have no wish to hold it back. What I say to you is God's truth, every word of it. Thank you, you can put the glass beside me here, and I'll put my lips to it if I am dry."

                                                          「fair-spoken」：言辞恳切的。

                                                          「bear no grudge」：不存怨恨。

                                                          「above-board」：光明正大的。

                                                          「hold back」：隐瞒 。

                                                          「God's truth」：真实的事 。

                                                          斯茂对其他警官都表现出十分嘲讽厌恶的样子，但是却对福尔摩斯另眼相看，前面又称赞福尔摩斯十分“clever”，他说福尔摩斯十分真诚，非常公平公正。知米妞觉得他这种想法或许是发自内心的，也可能是希望通过夸赞福尔摩斯希望他可以网开一面，替自己说话。那么各位读者是怎么认为的呢？

                                                          大意：“啊，先生，还是您说的话公平合理，虽然说我应当感谢您给我戴上了手镯。可是，我并不怨恨⋯⋯这都是光明磊落，公公正正的。您如果愿意听我的故事，我决不隐瞒，我所要说的句句都是实话。谢谢您，请把杯子搁在我身旁，我口渴的时候会把嘴唇靠近杯子来喝的。”


                                                          10. At last, however, when I was about eighteen, I gave them no more trouble, for I got into a mess over a girl and could only get out of it again by taking the Queen's shilling and joining the Third Buffs（步兵三团）, which was just starting for India.  

                                                          「for...」：表示理由或原因,意为“因为、由于”。

                                                          「got into a mess over」：谈恋爱。

                                                          「get out of」：摆脱;逃避

                                                          「taking the Queen's shilling 」：就是同意担任皇家海军或英国陆军的水手或士兵。

                                                          「starting for 」：出发，启程。

                                                          「which was just starting for India」：修饰“the Third Buffs”意为要去印度的步兵三团。

                                                          大意：在十八岁的时候因为恋爱出了麻烦，为了不给家里添麻烦，只好另谋生路。当时碰巧步兵三团就要调往印度，为了脱身，我就入伍了，选择了靠吃军饷为生的路。


                                                          11.What with the shock and the loss of blood, I fainted, and should have been drowned if Holder had not caught hold of me and paddled for the bank.

                                                          「What with the shock and the loss of blood, I fainted」：可以把它理解为：What with the shock and the loss of blood is that I fainted.

                                                          「should have been done ... if...」：由if引导的条件从句，表示与过去事实相反的假设。句型：条件从句（过去完成时）+主句（should/ would have +过去分词），这里将if条件从句放在了主句后面也是一样的意义。

                                                          e.g.If she had come yesterday,I should /would have told her about it.如果她昨天来了的话，我就会告诉她这件事了。

                                                          大意：由于惊吓和失血，我晕了过去，如果没有侯德抓着我向岸边游去的话我就会被淹死了。
