                                                                                                            熟词生义

                                                                                                            1. 「moon」: 本意是指“月亮”，加了s的moon在文中则表示“月份”啦~

                                                                                                            原文：These are the seven entrances to the home under the ground, for which Hook has been searching in vain these many moons.


                                                                                                            2. 「heave」: 本意是“举起”，在本文中“heave a sigh”搭配起来就是指“发出叹息”，

                                                                                                            原文：Hook heaved a heavy sigh

                                                                                                            e.g. Mr. Collier heaved a sigh and got to his feet. 科利尔先生深深叹了一口气，然后站起身。


                                                                                                            3. 「tear」: 本意是作名词，表示“眼泪”，在文章中的意思是做动词表示“撕碎”

                                                                                                            原文：Oh, I'll tear him!

                                                                                                            e.g. Be careful not to tear the paper. 小心不要把纸撕破了。


                                                                                                            4. 「hollow」: 原本是指“空心的，中空的”。文中除了用到’hollow trunk’这个表达，还在形容Hook笑的时候也用到了这个词，形容笑容干巴巴的：a hollow laugh or voice makes a weak sound and is without emotion

                                                                                                            原文：He laughed, but in a hollow way.

                                                                                                            e.g. a hollow laugh 干笑

                                                                                                            句子解析

                                                                                                            1. With the exception of Nibs, who has darted away to reconnoitre（侦查）[look around], they are already in their home under the ground, a very delightful residence／ of which we shall see a good deal presently.

                                                                                                            「with the exception of」：除了...

                                                                                                            「a very delightful residence」：一个美妙的住处，补充说明前面的home～

                                                                                                            孩子们都跑去哪儿了呢？原来，除了（with the exception of）Nibs——他跑到别处（darted away）侦察敌情去了——他们全都回到了地下的家里，那真是个美妙的住处（a very delightful residence），下面我们就要细说（we shall see a good deal presently）。


                                                                                                            2. "Shall I after him, Captain," asked pathetic Smee, "and tickle him with Johnny Corkscrew?" Smee had pleasant names for everything, and his cutlass（短弯刀） was Johnny Corkscrew, because he wiggled it in the wound.

                                                                                                            「tickle…with…」:  以…引某人发笑，例如：tickle her with jokes，讲笑话逗她开心

                                                                                                            「Johnny Corkscrew」：corkscrew其实就是我们用的开瓶器↓↓↓


                                                                                                            「pleasant name」: 好听的名字

                                                                                                            「wiggled it in the wound」：wiggle是扭动，wiggle it in the wound就是说用它（cutlass）在伤口里扭动。啊，这种场景想想都肉疼，该是多么残暴的一个海盗！

                                                                                                            这段就是说“我可以去追他吗，船长？”可怜巴巴的斯密问，“我可以用我的约翰开瓶钻给他挠痒痒吗？”斯密喜欢给什么东西都起一个好听的名字，他管他的短弯刀叫约翰开瓶钻，因为他喜欢用刀在伤口里旋转。


                                                                                                            3. "Ay," the captain answered. "if I was a mother I would pray to have my children born with this instead of that," and he cast a look of pride upon his iron hand and one of scorn upon the other.

                                                                                                            「If I was (were)…I would…」: 这里是虚拟语气的用法啦，Hook队长其实应该用were而不是was, 因为（敲黑板啦！！！）虚拟语气在表示与现在事实相反的情况下：

                                                                                                            从句：if 主语+过去式（Be动词用were）

                                                                                                            主句：主语+should/would/could/might do

                                                                                                            「have my children born with…」: 让我的小孩一出生就带着……

                                                                                                            「cast…upon…」: 向……投去

                                                                                                            看来Captain Hook对于自己的铁钩手臂很是得意！“是啊，”船长回答说，“我要是个妈妈，我一定祈求我的孩子生下来有这件东西，而不是那件东西。”他得意地瞄了一眼他的那只铁钩，又轻蔑地瞄了一眼他的那只手。


                                                                                                            4. "Ay," he said, "that's the fear that haunts me."

                                                                                                            「haunt」: 这个词表示（令人不快的事情）萦绕在心头：If something unpleasant haunts you, you keep thinking or worrying about it over a long period of time.

                                                                                                            举个例子：He would always be haunted by that scene in Well Park. 他将不断回想起威尔公园的那一幕。

                                                                                                            多亏鳄鱼吃了个闹钟，才能每次都让Captain Hook在鳄鱼靠近自己的时候提前就能反应过来。总有一天闹钟也会停的呀！这正是令Hook日日夜夜都担心的事（that's the fear that haunts me）


                                                                                                            5. Hook nodded. He stood for a long time lost in thought, and at last a curdling smile lit up his swarthy face. Smee had been waiting for it. "Unrip your plan, captain," he cried eagerly.

                                                                                                            「lost in thought」: 陷入沉思

                                                                                                            「curdling smile」: 冻结的微笑

                                                                                                            「lit up」: 点亮

                                                                                                            「swarthy face」：黝黑的面孔

                                                                                                            「Unrip your plan」：亮出你的计划

                                                                                                            胡克点了点头，他站着，凝神思考了好一阵子，一丝冻结的微笑浮现在他黝黑的脸上。斯密等着他发话。“亮出你的计划来吧，船长。”斯密急切地喊道。


                                                                                                            6. They began the verse, but they never finished it, for another sound broke in and stilled them. There was at first such a tiny sound that a leaf might have fallen on it and smothered it, but as it came nearer it was more distinct.

                                                                                                            「stilled」：在这里做及物动词，表示“使……平静下来”

                                                                                                            「smother」: 使窒息，抑制

                                                                                                            「distinct」: 清楚的

                                                                                                            他们开始唱起了这首歌，可是再也没能把它唱完，因为响起了另外一个声音，止住了他们的歌。起初，那声音很小，掉下一片树叶，就能把它盖住；但是离得越近，就越清晰。

                                                                                                             

                                                                                                            （附一张把Hook船长吓得没命的鳄鱼本尊）


                                                                                                            7. Once more the boys emerged into the open; but the dangers of the night were not yet over, for presently Nibs rushed breathless into their midst, pursued by a pack of wolves. The tongues of the pursuers were hanging out; the baying of them was horrible.

                                                                                                            「open」: 在这里作名词指“地面上”

                                                                                                            「rushed breathless into」: 气喘吁吁地冲进……

                                                                                                            「a pack of」: 一群（野兽等）

                                                                                                            「baying」: 指的就是狼的嚎叫声

                                                                                                            孩子们又回到地面上来了，可是，夜间的危险还没有完，忽然间尼布斯气喘吁吁地跑到他们那儿，后面追着一群狼，舌头吐得老长，嚎叫声好不吓人。


                                                                                                            8. It was a high compliment to Peter that at that dire moment their thoughts turned to him.

                                                                                                            「at that dire moment」: 在千钧一发之际

                                                                                                            在这种千钧一发的时刻，他们不由得都想到了（turn to）彼得，这应该算是对彼得最高的赞誉啦。


                                                                                                            9. The next moment is the long one, but victory came quickly, for as the boys advanced upon them in the terrible attitude, the wolves dropped their tails and fled.

                                                                                                            「advance upon」: 朝着……前进

                                                                                                            大家都弯下腰来从两腿间朝狼前进，随后的时间显得很长，可是胜利来得很快，孩子们用这种可怕的姿势朝着狼逼进时，那群狼全都耷拉着尾巴逃之夭夭了。
