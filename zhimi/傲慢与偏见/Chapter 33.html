And with regard to the resentment of his family, or the indignation of the world, if the former were excited by his marrying me, it would not give me one moment's concern—and the world in general would have too much sense to join in the scorn."
                                                      伊丽莎白说：“目前这件事情谈不到什么天良、廉耻、恩义。我跟达西先生结婚，并不触犯这些原则。要是他跟我结了婚，他家里人就厌恶他，那我毫不在乎；至于说天下人都会生他的气，我认为世界上多的是知义明理的人，不见得个个都会耻笑他。”

Do not imagine, Miss Bennet, that your ambition will ever be gratified. I came to try you. I hoped to find you reasonable; but, depend upon it, I will carry my point."

                                                            熟词生义

                                                            1.「colour」：前面的章节中有提到过“colour”作名词时的用法。本章中它作为动词，又是什么意思了呢？最基本的它可以表示“填色，着色”。由这个意思衍生出来，也可以表示“为…染色”，例如染发，染红天空。原文中出现的“colour”则意为“脸红”，用来形容Elizabeth听闻Catherine夫人到访的真正目的后诧异又厌恶，满脸涨的通红。

                                                            原文：colouring with astonishment and disdain.

                                                            e.g. Her eyes suddenly met his and she coloured slightly.

                                                            她的目光突然和他相遇，她微微有点脸红了。


                                                            2.「match」：“match”作为名词也是释义多多。常见的意思有“比赛，竞赛”；“适配，匹配”；“对手，敌手”。而如果你留心的话，在许多童话中它也有出现意为“火柴”，最著名的就是“卖火柴的小女孩”了，“The Little Match Girl”。但是呢，本章中出现的“match”表示“婚姻”，Catherine夫人一口咬定Elizabeth和Darcy的婚姻绝不能允许。

                                                            原文：This match, to which you have the presumption to aspire, can never take place..

                                                            e.g. They’re a perfect match.

                                                            他们是非常般配的一对。


                                                            3.「throw」：这个单词一般作动词表示“投掷，抛”。原文中用到“be thrown open”，表示“粗暴地推开”。“throw”在这里意思是“粗鲁地/猛烈地推”。

                                                            原文：till the door was thrown open and their visitor entered.

                                                            e.g. The bus stopped suddenly and we were all thrown forwards。

                                                            公共汽车猛地停住，我们都往前栽。

                                                            句子解析

                                                            1.  One morning, about a week after Bingley's engagement with Jane had been formed, as he and the females of the family were sitting together in the dining-room, their attention was suddenly drawn to the window, by the sound of a carriage.

                                                            「Bingley’s engagement with Jane」：Bingley和Jane的婚约。“engagement”在这里是“婚约”的意思。果然有情人终成眷属， Jane和Bingley历经坎坷还是走到了一起～

                                                            e.g. Tony was stunned when Lisa suddenly broke off their engagement.

                                                            托尼对莉萨突然解除他俩的婚约感到震惊。

                                                            「their attention was suddenly drawn to the window」：他们立刻被窗外的声音吸引过去。“draw attention to”即“对…注意”，“draw”在这里意为“使…注意”。

                                                            e.g. By letting the light draw attention to their happy faces, we are less focused on the grime of their clothing.

                                                            在光线的作用下，观众被孩子们快乐的脸部所吸引，而不会那么注意她们污浊的衣服。

                                                            大意是： 有一天早上，大约是Bingley和Jane订婚之后的一个星期，Bingley正和女眷们坐在饭厅里，忽然听到一阵马车声，大家都走到窗口去看。

                                                            （Bennet一家都来到窗边）


                                                            2. They both set off, and the conjectures of the remaining three continued, though with little satisfaction, till the door was thrown open and their visitor entered. It was Lady Catherine de Bourgh.

                                                            「They both set off」： 他们俩走了。“set off”表示“动身，出发”。Bingley和Jane刚确定了关系，还不想过早地被他人知道，便先行回避。

                                                            e.g. The president's envoy set off on another diplomatic trip.

                                                            总统特使再次踏上外交之旅。

                                                            「till the door was thrown open and their visitor entered」： 知道门被猛地推开，客人走进屋来。“be thrown open”在之前的熟词生义提到过，原文中形容Catherine夫人非常无礼地推门而入。一方面Catherine夫人认为Elizabeth会坏了自己女儿和Darcy的好事心有不满，另一方面她也是打心底里看不起Bennet一家。

                                                            大意是： 他们俩走了以后，另外三人依旧在那里猜测，可惜猜不出这位来客是谁。最后门开了，客人走进屋来，原来是Catherine de Bourgh夫人。


                                                            3. "Miss Bennet," replied her ladyship, in an angry tone, "you ought to know, that I am not to be trifled with. But however insincere you may choose to be, you shall not find me so.

                                                            「in an angry tone」：以气愤的口吻。Elizabeth说到这里已经有些难忍怒气。“in a adj. tone”表示以…的口气。例如“in a pleading tone”即“用恳求的口气”，“in a commanding tone”即“用命令的口气”。

                                                            「I am not to be trifled with」：我是不接受别人跟我开玩笑的。“trifle with”表示“玩弄；视同儿戏”。Catherine夫人对Elizabeth的回答非常不满，重申了自己的立场。

                                                            e.g. Don't trifle with the girl's affections.

                                                            不要玩弄那个女孩的感情。

                                                            「you shall not find me so」：我可不是那样的。“find sb. adj.” 意思是“发现某人处于某种状态”。Catherine夫人带着股傲慢说道，暗指“我可不会像你这样没有礼貌”。

                                                            e.g. I found myself nervous with him.

                                                            我发现自己面对他会很紧张。

                                                            大意是：夫人一听此话，很是生气：“Bennet小姐，你要知道，我是决不肯让人家来跟我开玩笑的。尽管你怎样不老实，我可不是那样。

                                                            （Catherine夫人开始质问Elizabeth）


                                                            4. My character has ever been celebrated for its sincerity and frankness, and in a cause of such moment as this, I shall certainly not depart from it.

                                                            「celebrated for its sincerity and frankness」：因其真诚坦白而被夸赞。“celebrate”在这里是可不是庆祝的意思哦，应理解为“赞美，赞扬”。Catherine夫人还是挺自恋的嘛，自己夸其自家来很有一套说辞。

                                                            e.g. poems that celebrate the joys of love.

                                                            赞美爱之喜悦的诗歌。

                                                            「in a cause of such moment as this」： 何况遇到现在这桩事。Catherine夫人也是好不掩饰，直接挑明了自己要剪断Elizabeth和Darcy姻缘的决心。

                                                            大意是： 我是个有名的老实坦白的人，何况遇到现在这桩事，我当然更要老实坦白。


                                                            5. "Miss Bennet, do you know who I am? I have not been accustomed to such language as this. I am almost the nearest relation he has in the world, and am entitled to know all his dearest concerns.".  

                                                            「I have not been accustomed to such language as this」： 我可从没听过这样不成体统的话。“be accustomed to”表示“习惯于，适应于”，相当于“be used to”。注意后面跟动词的话要用动名词形式！Catherine夫人句句都是戳在Elizabeth的痛点上，妄图以自己的高姿态给对方压力。

                                                            e.g. You may be accustomed to having a sense of security based on the stability of predictable interactions with people close to you.

                                                            当与靠近你的人有了可预见性的互动的稳定性之后，你会渐渐适应，并有了安全感。

                                                            「I am entitled to know all his dearest concerns」：我有权过问他一切的切身大事。“be entitled to”意思是“有…的资格”。Catherine夫人自诩为Darcy“最亲近的亲戚”，给了自己过问Darcy终身大事的权力。然而这是否属实就不得而知了～

                                                            e.g. You may be entitled to reclaim some of the tax you paid last year.

                                                            你或许有权要求退回去年你交付的部分税金。

                                                            大意是： Bennet小姐，你知道我是谁吗？你这种话真讲得不成体统。我差不多是他最亲近的长辈，我有权利过问他一切的切身大事。

                                                            （Catherine夫人急切地想听到答案）


                                                            6. "Miss Bennet I am shocked and astonished. I expected to find a more reasonable young woman. But do not deceive yourself into a belief that I will ever recede. I shall not go away till you have given me the assurance I require."

                                                            「do not deceive yourself into a belief」：别自己骗自己了 。“deceive yourself into belief”表示“自认为…”。Catherine夫人异常蛮横无理，寸步不让。

                                                            e.g. He would trick the fish deceive into trusting him.

                                                            它要使诡计让所有的鱼信任它。

                                                            「till you have given me the assurance I require」： 直到你答应我的要求 。“assurance”表示“担保，保证”。Elizabeth面对强势的Catherine夫人也丝毫没有退却，这让对方急了起来。Catherine夫人可能自己没察觉到，说出这样的话已经显得非常无礼。

                                                            大意是： Bennet小姐，我真是又惊骇又诧异。我没有料到你是这样一个不讲理的小妮子。可是你千万把头脑放清楚一点，别以为我会让步。你不答应我的要求我是不会走的。


                                                            7. "I have said no such thing. I am only resolved to act in that manner, which will, in my own opinion, constitute my happiness, without reference to you, or to any person so wholly unconnected with me."

                                                            「only resolved to act in that manner」：我自有主张。“resolved to”即“决心做…”。

                                                            e.g. I resolved to visit my grandmother at least once a year.

                                                            我决定每年至少要去看望祖母一次。

                                                            「without reference to you」：你管不了。在这里“reference”意思是“参考标准，参照物”。Catherine夫人一直强调自己礼节多么到位，而Elizabeth直接回击，表示自己自有主张，不需要向别人效仿或是学习。

                                                            e.g. She used her work experience as a frame of reference for her teaching.

                                                            她把自己的工作经历作为教学的参考依据。

                                                            大意是： 我并没有说这种话。我自有主张，怎样做会幸福，我就决定怎么样做，你管不了，任何像你这样的局外人也都管不了。

                                                            （Elizabeth强硬的回复）
