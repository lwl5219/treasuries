【1】 The subjection(服从) in which his father had brought him up had given him originally great humility of manner; 「The subjection in which his ......」：前半句是一个包含后置定语的句子，还是先找主干，“The subjection ... had given him ... humility of manner”, “in which”就是一个修饰“subjection”的定从，这种服从中，他父亲将他抚养大，碰到介词+which的这种从句，要是有同学看不懂可以尝试还原原语序哦 but it was now a good deal counteracted(抵消) by the self-conceit of a weak head, living in retirement, and the consequential feelings of early and unexpected prosperity. 「it was now a good deal counteracted by the self-conceit of ......」：极大地为自负所抵消了。“of”后面的短语都是解释形容“self-conceit”的，同学们读长句的时候一定要分清逻辑合理断句才能读懂意思哦~ 「the consequential feelings of early and unexpected prosperity」：因为年纪轻轻得到了一笔意外之财所带来的感觉 大意：父亲的严厉管教使他养成了唯唯诺诺的习气，但是这种习气如今又给大大抵消了，因为他本来就是个蠢材，一下子过上了悠闲生活，难免会飘飘然起来，何况年纪轻轻就发了意外之财，自然会越发自命不凡。

          【11】 In pompous nothings on his side, and civil assents on that of his cousins, their time passed till they entered Meryton. The attention of the younger ones was then no longer to be gained by him. 「pompous nothings」：浮夸的废话 「civil assents」：客套的赞同 大意：他一路上，他只管夸夸其谈，废话连篇，表妹们只得客客气气地随声附和，就这样来到了麦里屯。 这句话初读一遍有些同学会有点懵：这前面这么长的半句说的啥玩意儿？对于这些看这种句子容易懵的，告诉你一个好方法就是找结构，断句。英语是形合式语言，非常注重结构，像这句的前两小句结构就非常对称，在这给出一个断句版： In// pompous nothings/ on his side,/ and civil assents /on that of his cousins,// their time passed till they entered Meryton. //一大断，/一小断。这样看来，这句话大致结构是：在（......）中，时间就这样过去了，他们到了麦里屯。 所谓的前两小句结构对称，就是都是XX on XX’ s side, “on that of his cousins”，“that”指代“side”，这里就是“on cousins’ side”，这两小句理解一下就是，Mr. Collins这边说些浮夸的废话，他的表妹们这边客套的赞同 Their eyes were immediately wandering up in the street in quest of the officers, and nothing less than a very smart bonnet indeed, or a really new muslin(细平布) in a shop window, could recall them.

                      Pride and Prejudice  Chapter 10 内容精讲

                      熟词生义

                      1.「gain」：这个词我们经常会用它“获得，收获”的意思，其实作动词时，它还有“到达某地”的意思，to reach a place

                      原文：Kitty and Lydia, determined if possible to find out, led the way across the street, under pretense of wanting something in an opposite shop, and fortunately had just gained the pavement when the two gentlemen, turning back, had reached the same spot.

                      e.g. At last she gained the shelter of the forest. 她终于到达了森林中的隐蔽处。


                      2.「arrest」：平常最常见的意思就是“逮捕，拘捕”的意思，但是它还有一个“吸引（注意）”的意思哦，其实也挺好理解的，好像是“逮捕”了某人的注意力似的，to make sb notice sth and pay attention to it

                      原文：Mr. Darcy corroborated it with a bow, and was beginning to determine not to fix his eyes on Elizabeth, when they were suddenly arrested by the sight of the stranger ......

                      e.g. An unusual noise arrested his attention . 一阵不寻常的嘈杂声引起了他的注意。


                      3.「very」：“很，非常”的意思都知道，但是只有部分同学知道它还有形容词“（特指人或事物）正是的，恰好的，同一的”这样一个意思，能够起一个强调作用，used to emphasize that you are talking about a particular thing or person and not about another，细心的同学会发现原文中出现了可不止一次哦

                      原文：The officer was the very Mr. Denny concerning whose return from London Lydia came to inquire, and he bowed as they passed.

                      e.g. That's the very thing I need. 那正是我需要的东西。


                      4.「relate」：通常我们知道的它的意思是“（把…）联系起来”，其实它还有“讲，叙述（故事等）”的意思，即to give a spoken or written report of sth; to tell a story

                      原文：As they walked home, Elizabeth related to Jane what she had seen pass between the two gentlemen.

                      e.g. He related the facts of the case to journalists. 他给记者们讲述了这件事的实际情况。

                      句子解析

                      1. The subjection in which his father had brought him up had given him originally great humility of manner; but it was now a good deal counteracted by the self-conceit of a weak head, living in retirement, and the consequential feelings of early and unexpected prosperity.

                      「The subjection in which his ......」：前半句是一个包含后置定语的句子，还是先找主干，“The subjection ... had given him ... humility of manner”, “in which”就是一个修饰“subjection”的定从，这种服从中，他父亲将他抚养大，碰到介词+which的这种从句，要是有同学看不懂可以尝试还原原语序哦

                      「it was now a good deal counteracted by the self-conceit of ......」：极大地为自负所抵消了。“of”后面的短语都是解释形容“self-conceit”的，同学们读长句的时候一定要分清逻辑合理断句才能读懂意思哦~

                      「the consequential feelings of early and unexpected prosperity」：因为年纪轻轻得到了一笔意外之财所带来的感觉

                      大意：父亲的严厉管教使他养成了唯唯诺诺的习气，但是这种习气如今又给大大抵消了，因为他本来就是个蠢材，一下子过上了悠闲生活，难免会飘飘然起来，何况年纪轻轻就发了意外之财，自然会越发自命不凡。


                      2. The respect which he felt for her high rank, and his veneration for her as his patroness, mingling with a very good opinion of himself, of his authority as a clergyman, and his right as a rector, made him altogether a mixture of pride and obsequiousness, self-importance and humility.

                      「mingling with a very good opinion of himself」：“mingle with”，与......混合，“a very good opinion of himself”，对自己的自视甚高。

                      「of his authority as a clergyman, and his right as a rector」：这一小句的“of”其实是和前面的“himself”共用的一个“a very good opinion”，意思也就是说，对他自己作为牧师的权威，作为教区长的权利的自视甚高

                      「a mixture of pride and obsequiousness, self-importance and humility」：混合了傲慢与谄媚，自负与谦卑

                      大意：他一方面敬仰Lady Catherine的崇高地位，尊崇她作为自己的女赞助人，另一方面又非常看重自己，珍惜自己作教士的权威，作教区长的权利，这一切造就了他一身兼有傲慢与恭顺、自负与谦卑的双重性格。


                      3. This was his plan of amends—of atonement—for inheriting their father's estate; and he thought it an excellent one, full of eligibility and suitableness, and excessively generous and disinterested on his own part.

                      「plan of amends—of atonement」：赔罪的计划，赎罪的计划

                      「full of eligibility and suitableness」：非常的妥善得体

                      「excessively generous and disinterested on his own part」极其慷慨又无私

                      大意：这就是他所谓补偿的计划，赎罪的计划，为的是将来继承她们父亲的遗产时可以问心无愧。他认为这是个绝妙的办法，既妥善得体，又来得慷慨豪爽。

                      Mr. Collins真不愧是个被盖章“absurd”的人啊，他继承了人家房产，想娶一位他家的漂亮女儿，还要感叹自己慷慨，唉，荒谬！


                      4. The next morning, however, made an alteration; for in a quarter of an hour's tete-a-tete with Mrs. Bennet before breakfast, a conversation beginning with his parsonage-house, and leading naturally to the avowal of his hopes, that a mistress might be found for it at Longbourn, produced from her, amid very complaisant smiles and general encouragement, a caution against the very Jane he had fixed on.

                      这是一个完整的句子，太长了啊有木有？！淡定淡定，来慢慢看~

                      「made an alteration」：换了一个选择。上文提到过，Mr. Collins看到Jane“那张可爱的脸蛋儿”更确信要娶应该先娶长女的陈旧观念。所以Mr. Collins原来的第一选择是Jane，现在他换了

                      「for in a quarter of an hour's tete-a-tete」：这里的“for”是因为的意思哦，大家应该都能分辨吧。意思是因为在一刻钟的窃窃私语中......

                      「the avowal of his hopes」：公开了自己的心愿。那这个心愿是什么呢？就是后面这个“that”的从句，即要在Bennet家娶一位做女主人

                      「produced from her, amid ... , a caution against ......」：这里的produced from her其实是被提前了，正常的语序应该是a caution against the very Jane he had fixed on (was) produced from her, 意思是Mrs. Bennet给出了一条关于Jane的提醒建议，他之前恰好选择了Jane(作为结婚对象）。“amid very complaisant smiles and general encouragement”，在她的殷切微笑和慷慨鼓励中。

                      大意：不过第二天早上他又变更了主张，因为他和Mrs. Bennet亲密地交谈了一刻钟，先是谈起了他的牧师住宅，后来自然而然地把自己的心愿招供了出来，说是要在朗博恩找一位太太，而且要在她的令媛们中间找一位。Mrs. Bennet亲切地笑着，而且一再鼓励他，不过谈到他选定了Jane，她就不免要提醒他注意一下了。


                      5. Mr. Collins was to attend them, at the request of Mr. Bennet, who was most anxious to get rid of him, and have his library to himself; for thither Mr. Collins had followed him after breakfast.

                      「attend」：也是讲解过哦，在这不作“出席”的意思，而是“陪同”

                      「at the request of Mr. Bennet」：在Mr. Bennet的要求之下

                      「for thither Mr. Collins had followed him after breakfast」：“for”作连词，表原因，“因为”，“thither”，“到那里”，这句话就是说因为Mr. Collins在早饭之后就跟着Mr.Bennet到了书房。

                      大意：Mr. Bennet一心想把Mr. Collins支开，好独自待在书房里，便请他陪女儿们一道去。原来，吃过早饭以后，Mr. Collins就跟着他来到了书房，一直赖着不走。


                      6. In pompous nothings on his side, and civil assents on that of his cousins, their time passed till they entered Meryton.

                      「pompous nothings」：浮夸的废话

                      「civil assents」：客套的赞同

                      这句话初读一遍有些同学会有点懵：这前面这么长的半句说的啥玩意儿？对于这些看这种句子容易懵的，告诉你一个好方法就是找结构，断句。英语是形合式语言，非常注重结构，像这句的前两小句结构就非常对称，在这给出一个断句版：

                      In// pompous nothings/ on his side,/ and civil assents /on that of his cousins,// their time passed till they entered Meryton.

                      //一大断，/一小断。这样看来，这句话大致结构是：在（......）中，时间就这样过去了，他们到了麦里屯。

                      所谓的前两小句结构对称，就是都是XX on XX’ s side, “on that of his cousins”，“that”指代“side”，这里就是“on cousins’ side”，这两小句理解一下就是，Mr. Collins这边说些浮夸的废话，他的表妹们这边客套的赞同

                      大意：他一路上，他只管夸夸其谈，废话连篇，表妹们只得客客气气地随声附和，就这样来到了麦里屯。

                      朋友们，碰到初读不顺的句子，记得分析结构，断句哦~


                      7. The officer was the very Mr. Denny concerning whose return from London Lydia came to inquire, and he bowed as they passed.

                      「concerning whose return from London Lydia came to inquire」：跟在Mr. Denny后，那肯定是修饰Mr. Denny的咯，“concerning”，介词，“关于”的意思，concerning+whose，碰到这种介词+引导词的，看不懂的筒子们可以试着将先行词代入还原语序，“Lydia came to inquire concerning whose(Mr. Denny’s) return from London”

                      这样这个句子就好懂了，这位军官正是Mr. Denny, 怎么样的Mr. Denny呢，Lydia过来要询问是否从伦敦回来了没的Mr. Denny。

                      大意：这位军官就是Mr. Denny，Lydia跑来专要打听他从伦敦回来了没有。


                      8. The introduction was followed up on his side by a happy readiness of conversation—a readiness at the same time perfectly correct and unassuming.

                      「was followed up by」：由......跟随

                      「a happy readiness of conversation」：愉快的对于谈话的愿意

                      大意：一经介绍之后，他就高高兴兴、恳恳切切地谈起话来——既恳切，又显得非常正派，而且有有分寸。

                      Mr. Wickham出场了，他身上又有什么故事呢？


                      9.                       「was beginning to ... , when they were ...」：“be doing sth, when ......”，正在做......，这时......

                      「Elizabeth happening to see ... , was all astonishment at ...」：“happen to”,“恰巧”，中间穿插了一个happening to ...，修饰的是Elizabeth, 恰好看见他俩表情的Elizabeth

                      大意：Mr. Darcy证明他没有说谎，同时还鞠了一躬。Darcy正打算把眼睛从Elizabeth身上移开，这时突然看见了那个陌生人。Elizabeth见到他们两人面面相觑的那副神情，感到万分惊奇。

                      10. but though Jane would have defended either or both, had they appeared to be in the wrong, she could no more explain such behaviour than her sister.
